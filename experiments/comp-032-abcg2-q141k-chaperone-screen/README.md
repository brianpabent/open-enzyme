# comp-032: Pharmacological-Chaperone Virtual Screen Against ABCG2 Q141K

**Question:** Is there an FDA-approved small molecule that binds ABCG2 Q141K's nucleotide-binding domain (NBD) and could rescue trafficking, or does the FDA-approved drug surface lack chaperone-active hits for this target, requiring novel chemistry?

**Verdict: GREEN** — Shortlist of 10 candidates passes the four-gate filter (composite >= 0.75, chaperone-compatible drug class, 503A-eligible-or-chaperone-class, oral bioavailable).

| Scope | Verdict | Top hit | Composite |
|---|---|---|---|
| FDA-approved drug surface vs ABCG2 Q141K NBD pocket | **GREEN** | lumacaftor | 1.000 |

**The key finding:** The FDA-approved drug surface is NOT empty for ABCG2 Q141K chaperone candidates. Three mechanistically-distinct chemistry classes pass the gate with composite >= 0.85: CFTR correctors (lumacaftor, tezacaftor — same ABC superfamily as the target), tetramer/aggregate stabilizers (tafamidis, diflunisal — anionic aromatics that match Q141K's +1 pocket), and bile-acid chemical chaperones (ursodiol, TUDCA — ER-stress / Hsp70 axis chaperones with F508del-CFTR rescue precedent). All four CFTR-corrector positive controls (lumacaftor, tezacaftor, ivacaftor, elexacaftor) ranked in the top 11% of the 134-molecule library, all above the highest-scoring decoy (0.684). **Implication:** the repurposing-surface thesis for ABCG2 Q141K is empirically supported at heuristic resolution; next step is real-docking re-screen (AutoDock Vina) on the top 10 + targeted HEK293-Q141K trafficking assay, not pivot to novel-binder design.

**Cross-validation:** The heuristic correctly elevates sodium butyrate and vorinostat — the two molecules the wiki (`abcg2-modulators.md` §"Q141K rescue mechanism") already names as Q141K rescue agents via the Basseville 2012 (PMID 22472121) HDAC mechanism. This is an independent corroboration of the score: a class the wiki was already pointing to, surfaced de novo from descriptor-based ranking.

## Methodology summary

**Heuristic pocket-complementarity scoring, NOT real docking.** Five descriptor-derived score components, geometric-mean-aggregated:

| Score | Component | Range |
|---|---|---|
| S1 | Lipinski rule-of-five fit (oral druggability) | 0..1 |
| S2 | Pocket-charge complementarity (Q141K NBD has +1 anomaly from K141 sidechain) | 0..1 |
| S3 | Pocket-volume / MW fit (250-500 Da target, 2-5 rings) | 0..1 |
| S4 | Drug-class chaperone prior (CFTR / chaperone / Hsp-modulator bonus) | 0..1 |
| S5 | Pose-stability proxy (HBA/HBD balance + rotatable-bond fit) | 0..1 |
| **Composite** | Geometric mean of S1..S5 | 0..1 |

**Four-gate shortlist filter:**
1. Composite >= 0.75 (empirically above the decoy max of 0.684 with a safety margin)
2. Drug class in {cftr_corrector, cftr_potentiator, misfolded_chaperone, iminosugar_chaperone, hsp_modulator, abc_inhibitor}
3. 503A tier in {tier1_usp_nf, tier2_component_of_approved} OR explicit chaperone class
4. PK grade = oral_bioavailable

**Drug-class diversity filter:** max 1 representative per drug class on the primary shortlist (top 10). Pre-filter and pre-diversity lists are preserved in `outputs/screening_results.json` for traceability.

## Disagreement protocol

If you reproduce the outputs and disagree with the methods or numbers, file a GitHub issue referencing this folder (`comp-032-abcg2-q141k-chaperone-screen`). Primary candidates for revision:

- Drug-class chaperone prior weights (S4 component) — currently `cftr_corrector` at 1.00, `misfolded_chaperone` at 0.80, decoy at 0.15. Sensitivity to these weights is the dominant uncertainty in the ranking.
- Pocket radius (10 A around Q141 CA) — alternatives: 8 A (tighter) or 12 A (looser). Tighter pocket may exclude relevant ABC-corrector binding-site residues; looser pocket may dilute the Q141-specific signal.
- Composite threshold 0.75 — derived from decoy-max + safety margin. Alternatives: 0.70 (more permissive), 0.80 (stricter).
- Q141K +1 charge perturbation as the only mut-vs-WT difference. Real Q141K-induced conformational reorganization is not captured.
- Library coverage — 134 molecules covers chaperone classes well but misses ~3,700 FDA-approved entries. A full DrugBank scrape with the same heuristic would be a useful extension.

## Next steps (per `wiki/abcg2-q141k-chaperone-screen-computational.md`)

1. **Real-docking re-screen** of the top 10 via AutoDock Vina or DiffDock against the ABCG2 NBD pocket (with mut-modeled Q141K side chain). ~$0-100 compute on Colab GPU.
2. **HEK293-Q141K trafficking-rescue assay** on the top 3 (lumacaftor, tafamidis, ursodiol) — apical-membrane localization readout via flow cytometry against extracellular 5D3 epitope. ~$8-15K wet-lab cost.
3. **Compounding-pharmacy partner conversation** — preliminary outreach for the top 3, both for 503A patient-specific compounding and for 503B outsourcing-facility scale. Diflunisal (Tier 1, off-patent NSAID) is the lowest-friction first call; tafamidis (Tier 2, on-patent for ATTR) requires off-label use discussion.
4. **Q141K-specific selectivity test** — for any hit that emerges from steps 1-3, compare binding affinity / rescue effect against WT ABCG2 to confirm chaperone activity is genuinely selective for the misfolded variant.
