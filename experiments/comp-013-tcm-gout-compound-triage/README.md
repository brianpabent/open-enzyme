# comp-013: TCM Gout Compound Triage

**Question:** Which Traditional Chinese Medicine (TCM) compounds with documented gout indication are mechanistically viable, when triaged via the comp-004 IC50 occupancy + comp-007 composite scoring frameworks?

**Verdict (top-line):** Of 9 candidate TCM compounds, 4 land **GUT-LUMINAL VIABLE** (luteolin, astilbin, emodin, berberine), 1 lands **MODERATE** (rhein, with dose caveat), and 4 land **MECHANISM UNCLEAR** (aucubin, cylindrin, chlorogenic acid, atractylenolide I) — primarily because no curated ChEMBL bioactivity data exists for them. The Si Miao San multi-herb formula has the strongest clinical evidence (24-RCT meta-analysis, SUA −90.62 µmol/L vs anti-inflammation control), though attribution to a single component is impossible.

## Methodology notes

**comp-004 IC50 occupancy framework** — applied directly. Same Hill equation n=1, same gut-conc model (dose × (1−BA) ÷ 250 mL, capped at intestinal solubility), same risk thresholds (LOW <0.5×, MODERATE 0.5-2×, HIGH 2-5×, VERY_HIGH ≥5×).

**comp-007 composite scoring — adapted**. Three changes:

1. **Gut-enrichment is BIDIRECTIONAL.** comp-007's HDACi candidates all targeted Q141K-ABCG2 in gut mucosa — a target where low BA is uniformly favorable. TCM compounds split: gut-luminal targets (favorable for low BA) vs systemic targets URAT1/XO/NLRP3 (UNFAVORABLE for low BA). Score is now site-aware.
2. **Selectivity uses on-target/off-target ratio.** comp-007 used HDAC6/class-I ratio (cardiotoxicity avoidance). Here, selectivity = best gout-relevant on-target IC50 ÷ best non-gout off-target IC50.
3. **Animal-model evidence is admissible.** comp-007 ranked on biochemical IC50 only. For TCM compounds, ChEMBL coverage is so sparse that animal in vivo dose-response data must count toward the verdict.

**Bioavailability layer** — explicitly load-bearing for TCM. Many compounds have impressive in vitro IC50s that cannot be achieved in vivo at supplement-grade dosing. Per-compound BA values cited in `inputs/compounds.json` with `[UNVERIFIED — bound: ...]` markers where primary source could not be anchored.
