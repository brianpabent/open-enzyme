# comp-029: Combined CP0 Systems Model — Dietary Rosmarinic Acid + Engineered DAF SCR1-4

**Question:** Does dietary rosmarinic acid (fluid-phase + gut-luminal C3 convertase inhibitor) combined with engineered DAF SCR1-4 (MSU-crystal-surface decay-accelerator) provide additive CP0 coverage at the predicted different geometric scales, with a combined-effect prediction range meaningfully larger than either intervention alone?

**Verdict: YELLOW** at all three accessibility priors. The combined median is 1.08-1.10× the better singleton (well below the 1.5× GREEN threshold) and the combined 95% CI overlaps both singleton 95% CIs. **The combined-strategy thesis is not refuted** (no interaction blocker → RED path closed) but the multiplicative-composition gain over each singleton is structurally capped because both arms individually saturate at their operative concentrations. Wet-lab co-administration arm in [`validation-experiments.md` §1.25](../../../validation-experiments.md) is **parked** pending wet-lab measurement of DAF SCR1-4 MSU-surface accessibility (the single load-bearing input that could collapse the prior uncertainty and either confirm GREEN or shift to RED).

## Methodology — two orthogonal models with explicit confidence bounds

Per the comp-029 brief and BioDesignBench evaluation-depth discipline ([`etc/autonomous-screening-methodology.md`](../../../etc/autonomous-screening-methodology.md) §"BioDesignBench evaluation-depth audit"), two orthogonal models are non-negotiable.

### Model 1 — Rosmarinic acid C3 convertase inhibition kinetics

- Hill function f_inhib = [RA] / (IC50 + [RA]), n = 1.
- IC50 sampled log-uniform on **[5 μM, 180 μM]** — the operative-mechanism range. Excludes 1500 μM C5-convertase-direct (Sahu 1999) because RA doesn't operate at that step. The 5 μM lower bound is Englberger 1988; the 180 μM upper bound is Sahu 1999 CP hemolytic; the 34 μM Sahu 1999 C3b-covalent-IC50 sits roughly mid-range.
- [RA] sampled per regime:
  - **Fluid-phase systemic:** free plasma RA log-uniform [5 nM, 100 nM] around the Baba 2004 Cmax (20 nM).
  - **Gut-luminal:** log-uniform [50 μM, 1100 μM] across the Kang 2021 calculated intestinal concentration range (252-1100 μM after 200 mg oral), extended down to 50 μM for dietary-tier dosing.
- Output: fraction of C3 convertase activity inhibited, as a distribution (5th-95th percentile + median).

### Model 2 — DAF SCR1-4 decay-accelerator kinetics on MSU surface

- Steady-state surface convertase occupancy SS = k_deposit / (k_intrinsic + k_DAF).
- k_intrinsic from Fischer 1981: C4b2a t₁/₂ = 7.5 min at 37°C.
- k_DAF = k_intrinsic × accel_factor; accel_factor = max_accel × [effective_DAF] / (half_sat + [effective_DAF]) with max_accel = 20× (membrane-DAF reduces t₁/₂ by 10-20× per Pangburn 1986 / Medof 1984), half_sat = 50 nM.
- [effective_DAF] = [DAF] × α, where:
  - [DAF] log-uniform [10 nM, 500 nM] (synovial effective concentration after engineered-koji secretion + delivery).
  - α ∈ {0.05, 0.20, 0.80} — three explicit accessibility priors. **This is the load-bearing wet-lab unknown** per comp-012's explicit Limitations section: "Whether the stalk-truncated SCR1-4 construct retains the ability to inhibit C3b deposition, accelerate CP/AP C3 convertase decay, or reduce C5a generation in a gut-lumen environment is a wet-lab question."
- Fraction decayed away = accel_factor / (1 + accel_factor).
- Output: per-α fraction of MSU-surface C3 convertase decayed, as a distribution.

### Cross-multiplication

The two interventions act at different geometric scales: RA on nascent C3 convertase assembly (fluid-phase / gut-luminal) and DAF on already-deposited surface convertases. **Default assumption: independent multiplicative composition** on residual fractions: f_combined = 1 - (1 - f_RA) × (1 - f_DAF). This is the standard treatment of two truly independent fractional reductions. The coupled-scenario sensitivity (modest super-additivity from RA reducing the substrate pool DAF then acts on) is also computed and reported — it does NOT change the YELLOW verdict.

## V1 simplifications (owned)

1. **Hill n=1 for both models.** No cooperativity parameter introduced; appropriate for first-pass mass-action enzyme inhibition + first-pass surface-decay-acceleration.
2. **Max DAF acceleration capped at 20×.** Membrane DAF achieves 10-20× per Pangburn 1986 / Medof 1984; soluble SCR1-4 lacking the GPI anchor probably achieves less, but we use the membrane-DAF upper bound as a conservative ceiling on the optimistic prior. If the wet-lab measurement shows soluble SCR1-4 only achieves 2-3× acceleration, the YELLOW verdict only deepens.
3. **Independent multiplicative composition as default.** Coupled-scenario sensitivity is reported as a second-order check; the verdict is invariant across the two.
4. **No explicit gut-luminal-to-MSU-surface mass transport modeling.** The Sahu 1999 IC50 is from a cell-surface C3b deposition assay, not an MSU-crystal-specific assay; we apply it to the MSU context as the closest published equivalent. An MSU-specific Sahu 1999 follow-up assay (Phase 2 follow-up flagged in comp-020) would resolve this.
5. **DAF half-saturation [DAF] of 50 nM** — order-of-magnitude prior; not a measured value for soluble SCR1-4 in the MSU-surface context.
