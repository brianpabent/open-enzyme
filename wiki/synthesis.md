## Contradictions Found

1. **The supplements stack pharmacologically antagonizes the engineered uricase platform by inhibiting ABCG2.** *Supported*.
   - *Locations:* `supplements-stack.md`, `abcg2-modulators.md`, `gut-lumen-sink.md`. `comp-004` (`supplement-abcg2-antagonism-computational.md`) provides the quantitative analysis.
   - *Analysis:* The engineered uricase platform relies on the ABCG2 transporter to secrete uric acid into the gut lumen. Several compounds in the stack — quercetin, curcumin, EGCG, and genistein (from soy) — are documented functional inhibitors of ABCG2 at concentrations achievable with standard supplement doses. For androgen-dominant or Q141K-positive users, whose ABCG2 function is already compromised, this creates a direct pharmacological conflict. The stack may be closing the very gate the engineered enzyme needs open. The new risk-tier stratification in `abcg2-modulators.md` helps, but the fundamental contradiction remains.
   - *Suggested Action:* Elevate the "inhibitor flavonoids" to a dedicated "Contraindicated with Uricase Platform" section in `supplements-stack.md`. Prioritize ABCG2 *inducers* like sulforaphane and fermentable fiber in any protocol that includes engineered uricase.

   > **Claude review — Confirmed.** The pharmacological antagonism is real and quantitatively anchored: comp-004 (`supplement-abcg2-antagonism-computational.md`) predicts 87–89% ABCG2 inhibition for quercetin and curcumin at supplement-grade doses via IC50 occupancy, and `abcg2-modulators.md §"The supplements-stack contradiction"` already tiers the risk by genotype × androgen status × dose. The Pass 2 synthesizer correctly names the Q141K + androgen-dominant intersection as highest-concern. Two nuances: (a) EGCG cannot be flatly labeled "contraindicated" — Yu 2024 (PMID 38757391) showed net-favorable in vivo ABCG2/URAT1/GLUT9 effect in hyperuricemic mice contradicting in vitro inhibition, and EGCG's mechanism is expression-level (72h) not acute transport block, so the 48h comp-004 framework explicitly does not apply; this is called out in the computational analysis itself. (b) The "Contraindicated with Uricase Platform" section language risks compliance collapse for the wild-type dietary-level majority where risk is near zero. Better framing: risk-tiered advisory (already in `abcg2-modulators.md`) rather than blanket contraindication.

2. **The shio-koji delivery format is incompatible with peptide payloads like carnosine or KPV.** *Supported*.
   - *Locations:* `koji-home-fermentation.md`, `engineered-koji-protocol.md` §15, `carnosine.md`, `kpv-peptide.md`.
   - *Analysis:* Shio-koji's 7–14 day room-temperature fermentation relies on active native proteases. These proteases will hydrolyze the exposed peptide bonds of small peptides like carnosine (a dipeptide) or KPV (a tripeptide), destroying their therapeutic activity. This is a hard biochemical constraint. Robusly folded globular proteins like uricase or lactoferrin may survive (see Experiment §1.10), but linear peptides will not.
   - *Suggested Action:* The format constraint table in `engineered-koji-protocol.md` §15 should be propagated to `carnosine.md` and `kpv-peptide.md`. The default delivery format for any peptide-expressing koji strain must be dried/heat-inactivated powder or a short-hold (cooked) format like amazake, not shio-koji.

   > **Claude review — Confirmed.** The biochemistry is unambiguous: shio-koji's 7–14 day room-temperature ferment maintains active native serine proteases (ALP, NPr), and dipeptides/tripeptides like carnosine and KPV have zero conformational protection — the peptide bonds are substrate, not structural. `koji-home-fermentation.md` and `engineered-koji-protocol.md §15` both carry this constraint, and `carnosine.md` §"Format constraint for koji delivery" explicitly states it. The contrast with folded globular proteins is mechanistically correct and comp-001 (uricase LOW verdict, 100% buried recognition sites) empirically supports uricase's likely survival. One refinement: lactoferrin's shio-koji survival is not yet as clean as the Pass 2 synthesizer implies — comp-005 returned HIGH (full sequence) / MODERATE (mature protein aa 20–710), with 3 ALP-exposed sites in the mature protein; §1.10's lactoferrin arm remains a feasibility gate, not a confirmation experiment. The Pass 2 synthesizer's phrasing "may survive" captures this appropriately.
