# The gut-lumen uricase question is unfinished. Open Enzyme is restarting it.

In September 2022, a Phase 2a trial of an oral, non-absorbed engineered uricase called ALLN-346 was terminated on the day it was scheduled to complete primary readout. Nineteen patients had been enrolled across seventeen US sites against a planned cohort of more than two hundred. The asset was Allena Pharmaceuticals' last gout program; the company wound down shortly afterward. The trial, NCT04987294, never delivered an efficacy result in the patient population it was designed to study.

The mechanism it was testing is still an open scientific question — and we think it is the **most important unanswered question in the gout-platform-design space.** Open Enzyme is restarting work on that question from a different angle, and we are looking for collaborators who know more about it than we do.

This post is part briefing, part call for help. If you are a clinician, biochemist, gut physiologist, regulatory expert, or anyone with lineage to the ALLN-346 program at Allena or Codexis, we would like to talk to you.

---

## What ALLN-346 was, and what it tested

ALLN-346 was an engineered variant of *Candida utilis* uricase — the enzyme that degrades uric acid into the more soluble allantoin (in most mammals; humans and great apes lost the gene ~20 million years ago, which is why we get gout and they don't). The variant carried seven mutations (I180V, V190G, Y165F, E51K, Q244K, I132R, A87G per US10815461B2) selected via Codexis's ProteinGPS directed-evolution platform. The result: a **20-fold improvement in protease half-life** in pancreatic juice (85 min vs 4 min for the wild-type enzyme), with catalytic activity preserved. In other words: an oral enzyme that survives the small intestine.

The therapeutic logic is mechanistically clean and worth restating because it is the foundation of multiple ongoing programs (ours included):

1. About **one-third of daily uric acid excretion** in healthy humans goes through the gut, secreted from the bloodstream into the intestinal lumen by the **ABCG2** transporter on apical enterocyte membranes. (The other two-thirds is renal.)
2. If you put a stable uricase **in the gut lumen**, it degrades that secreted uric acid before it can be reabsorbed — creating a concentration sink that pulls additional urate from blood, across the intestinal wall, and out of the body as allantoin (water-soluble, easily excreted).
3. Crucially: the enzyme **does not need to be absorbed**. It operates entirely in the intestinal lumen. That side-steps the immunogenicity problem that has plagued every IV uricase from rasburicase onward — pegloticase, the only marketed chronic uricase, has anti-drug antibody rates above 30% by month six and is restricted to refractory gout.

ALLN-346 was the first program to test this strategy in humans. The preclinical proof-of-concept (Pierzynowska et al., *Frontiers in Medicine* 2020, PMID 33330529) showed **44% plasma urate reduction** in URKO mice (uricase-knockout) over 7 and 19 days of oral dosing. The Phase 1 single-ascending-dose trial in healthy volunteers (NCT04236219, completed November 2020, N=24) confirmed no systemic absorption, no immune reactions, and no serious adverse events at any dose.

The Phase 2 clinical program then split into two pieces:

- **Study 201** (NCT04987242, completed March 2022, N=16) — gout patients with chronic kidney disease (CKD), already on stable allopurinol. **Result: statistically significant serum uric acid reduction on days 5–7 (p < 0.05).** The mechanism worked.
- **Study 202** (broader cohort, gout without CKD, completed July 2022) — **0–5% mean serum urate reduction at days 7 and 14, not statistically significant vs placebo.** The mechanism did not detectably work.

That is the divergence the program never got to resolve. The pivotal Phase 2a trial (NCT04987294) — designed to scale Study 201's CKD result across 17 US sites with more than 200 patients — was registered, started, and terminated on **September 2, 2022**, with 19 of the planned cohort enrolled. No further Allena gout trials were ever filed.

## Why the divergence is the most important question

The wiki at [openenzyme.org](https://github.com/brianpabent/open-enzyme) carries a one-line riskiest-assumption call generated by our multi-pass synthesis daemon yesterday. Quoting it directly:

> *The platform thesis's single most load-bearing belief that is least supported by the corpus is that the gut-lumen uricase sink will produce a clinically meaningful serum urate reduction in a typical gout population.*

Translation: every gut-lumen uricase platform on earth, including ours, depends on the assumption that what worked in CKD patients will also work in normal-renal-function patients. **That assumption has one human data point against it (Study 202) and zero in its favor.** Computational flux modeling we ran in March (comp-019, Monte Carlo n=5000 on ABCG2 kinetics from Nakayama 2011 and Miyazaki 2025's direct human jejunal urate-secretion measurements) predicts a genotype-robust −0.5 to −1.0 mg/dL effect at 25 mg/day uricase, but the model assumes the gut sink isn't compensated for by upregulated renal excretion. Whether the kidney compensates — and how much — is the load-bearing biological question.

The plausible failure mode is uncomfortable: **patients with normal renal function may have so much spare excretion capacity that removing 30% of the gut route triggers a renal compensation that nullifies the serum effect.** CKD patients lack that compensation reserve, which is why Study 201 saw signal. If that's the real story, the gut-lumen uricase mechanism is a **CKD-specific add-on, not a general gout treatment** — and the platform value proposition shrinks accordingly.

This is testable. It has not been tested.

## What Open Enzyme is doing differently

Open Enzyme is an open-source library of engineered, food-grade microbial strains that produce therapeutic enzymes. Our first target is uricase delivered via engineered koji (*Aspergillus oryzae*) — the GRAS-certified mold that has been fermenting Japanese soy sauce, miso, and sake for a thousand years. The genetic engineering needed to add a uricase cassette to koji is straightforward; the strain engineering is documented; the regulatory positioning sits within the existing food-fermentation framework rather than the drug-development framework.

The deliberate departures from ALLN-346:

1. **Living organism, not purified enzyme.** Koji secretes uricase continuously into the meal it's fermenting. The enzyme is replaced as it's degraded, lowering the per-unit-time stability requirement.
2. **Open-source genetics and protocols.** All construct designs, fermentation protocols, and assay methods are public. Anyone with a $50–500 home-fermentation setup can replicate.
3. **Stacked payloads on the same chassis.** Beyond uricase: lactoferrin (complement modulation), carnosine (URAT1/GLUT9 modulation), DAF/CD55 SCR1-4 (CP0 chokepoint coverage). The platform thesis treats the gut-lumen sink as one arm of a multi-mechanism intervention rather than a standalone drug.

But none of these design choices change the fundamental scientific question: **does the gut-lumen mechanism produce clinically meaningful serum urate reduction in typical gout patients?** Whether the enzyme arrives via Codexis-engineered protein, engineered koji, engineered *S. boulardii*, or engineered *E. coli* Nissle, the mechanism is the same and the validation gap is the same.

So Open Enzyme inherits ALLN-346's unfinished question. We can't pivot past it. We need to answer it.

## What we want to do, and where we need help

The validation path we're scoping has three layers:

### Layer 1: re-analysis of the existing ALLN-346 clinical data

Studies 201 and 202 each generated patient-level serum urate trajectories. Some of that data was published in conference abstracts (EULAR 2022) and SEC filings, but the per-patient time-series data has not been re-analyzed under the lens of "**did the gut effect get cancelled by renal compensation in non-CKD patients?**" This is a stratified-analysis question, not a new-trial question.

We are looking for: clinicians or biostatisticians who **had access to or can request access to** the raw Allena trial data. The asset is gone but the data should still exist somewhere — in former Allena hands, in CRO archives, or in regulatory filings. A re-analysis stratified by baseline eGFR, ABCG2 Q141K genotype (likely not collected — would be a flag), and dietary urate input could resolve the CKD-vs-normal-renal divergence question without running a new trial.

### Layer 2: a small-N self-experiment with engineered koji

Our nearest-term wet-lab plan is a single-subject (Brian's) self-experiment using engineered koji as a daily fermented food. Continuous SUA monitoring over a 90-day on/off/on protocol, with simultaneous urinary urate (FEUA), inflammatory markers (hs-CRP, IL-1β), and dietary tracking. This is n=1 — a proof-of-mechanism-in-Brian's-physiology, not a clinical readout. It cannot answer the population-level question, but it can detect whether Brian's specific physiology compensates and to what degree.

We are looking for: gut-physiology and clinical-pharmacology expertise to design the protocol rigorously, advise on confounders (especially diet-dependence of FEUA), and interpret the results with appropriate caveats.

### Layer 3: a properly-powered Phase 2b in non-CKD gout

This is the trial Allena couldn't run. It would need: typical-gout cohort (eGFR >60, no CKD), Q141K genotype stratification (which **no oral uricase trial has ever done** — every Phase 2 since rasburicase has missed this obvious pharmacogenomic axis), 25 mg/day equivalent uricase delivery, 12-week duration, sustained SUA endpoint. comp-019's flux model predicts the effect should be detectable if it exists; the trial should resolve the question definitively.

We are looking for: a clinical-trials team or pharma partner willing to revisit the question with explicit ABCG2 stratification. The mechanism is no longer a commercial speculation — it has a $50M+ failed program behind it and three live academic / startup programs (PRX-115 Protalix, SSS11 Shenyang Sunshine, the PULSE probiotic) carrying the same assumption forward without resolving it.

## Specific roles we want to fill

- **Gut microbiome / in vivo validation** — gnotobiotic mouse models, microbiome dynamics, engineered-strain colonization. We have the strain design; we need rigorous in vivo validation.
- **Pharma translation / regulatory strategy** — NF-κB / intestinal signaling, FDA / IND pathway. The food-fermentation positioning may sidestep parts of the drug-approval process; understanding which parts is load-bearing.
- **Innate immune safety** — TLR signaling, PAMP / epitope analysis. Engineered koji presents fungal cell-wall and secreted-enzyme epitopes that need careful safety review.
- **Anyone with ALLN-346 / Allena program lineage** — engineering, clinical operations, regulatory filings, business development. The asset is dead but the institutional knowledge isn't.
