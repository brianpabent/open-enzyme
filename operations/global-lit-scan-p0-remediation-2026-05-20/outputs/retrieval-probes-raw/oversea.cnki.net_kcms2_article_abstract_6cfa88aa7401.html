Rapid identification by UPLC-Q-TOF-MS of the chemical composition of Sanghuangporus vaninii alcohol extract from poplar and its urate-lowering activity and nephroprotective effect

English Full Text (MT) Chinese Full Text Attached video

LIU Tongzhang JIANG Wenwen LI Yueru

吉林农业大学中药材学院 珲春华瑞参业生物工程股份有限公司 农业部参茸产品质量监督检测中心

Abstract： The chemical composition of the Sanghuangporus vaninii alcohol extract from poplar was identified by UPLC-Q-TOF-MS， and the compound mass spectrometry data were collected in positive and negative ion modes. In vitro experiments were used to detect the inhibitory effect of Sanghuangporus vaninii alcohol extract on xanthine oxidase (XOD). Animal experiments were used to establish an in vivo model of hyperuricemia (HUA) mice by intragastric administration of potassium oxyzinate， and biochemical indexes such as uric acid (UA)， creatinine (CRE)， urea nitrogen (BUN)， xanthine oxidase activity and other biochemical indexes were measured， and tissues of kidney， liver and spleen were collected， and renal hematoxylin-eosin (HE) staining was used for histopathological examination. Combined with the retention time and related literature， a total of 42 compounds were identified from the Sanghuangporus vaninii flavonol extracts of poplar， including 11 pyrones， 9 organic acids， 8 fatty acids， 7 flavonoids， 3 phenols， 2 amino acids and 2 terpenoids. In vitro experiments showed that the Sanghuangporus vaninii alcohol extract of poplar had a certain inhibitory effect on XOD. The results of animal experiments showed that， compared with the allopurinol group， the serum levels of UA， BUN， CRE and XOD activities and the liver XOD activity in the middle and high-dose groups of poplar mulberry alcohol extract were reduced to varying degrees (P<0.05， P<0.01)， and pathological sections showed that the kidney damage was obvious in the allopurinol group and the degree of kidney injury in the Sanghuangporus vaninii alcohol extract group was reduced. Therefore， the Sanghuangporus vaninii alcohol extract may reduce the blood uric acid level in mice by inhibiting XOD activity， and has a certain anti-kidney injury effect. More Reset
