## Abstract-Level Signal

The visible CNKI abstract describes 22 hyperuricemia patients without severe cardiovascular, liver, kidney, lung, or other vital-organ disease taking *Cordyceps militaris* powder. After one month, serum uric acid, BUN, and creatinine were reported as significantly changed (`P < 0.05`) with "efficiency rate decreasing" values of UA 77.3%, BUN 61.9%, and CREA 85.7%.

This is discovery-positive but not promotion-grade evidence. The abstract does not expose dose, product specification, inclusion/exclusion criteria beyond severe-organ-disease exclusion, baseline values, endpoint definition for "efficiency rate," adverse-event tracking, or whether any comparator/control existed.
