## Verdict

This source clears the threshold for a cautious wiki evidence update:

- Evidence level: **Animal Model**.
- Material: *C. militaris* **whole water extract**, not purified cordycepin alone.
- Model: potassium oxonate plus yeast-paste hyperuricemia rat model.
- Best visible dose: `0.5 g/(kg*d)`.
- Serum urate effect: `281.62 -> 93.27 micromol/L`, reported inhibition rate `66.88%`.
- Mechanism claims visible on publisher page: renal URAT1/GLUT9 downregulation, OAT1/ABCG2 upregulation, hepatic XOD activity inhibition, renal oxidative-stress / inflammatory-marker reduction, and gut-microbiota diversity effects.
- Extract composition visible on publisher page: polysaccharides `35.86%`, protein `27.05%`, phenolics `0.21%`; table-visible nucleosides include cordycepin `0.83%`, adenosine `0.34%`, uridine `0.41%`, guanosine `0.21%`, and N6-(2-hydroxyethyl)adenosine `0.54%`.

## Boundaries

Do not overpromote this as human evidence. It is a rat model.

Do not collapse the finding into "cordycepin works" alone. The paper tests a whole water extract containing polysaccharides, protein, nucleosides, amino acids, and phenolics. Cordycepin is present, but the result is an extract-level result.

Do not describe this as an MSU-crystal flare model. The source supports renal NLRP3 / IL-1beta inflammatory-marker involvement in a hyperuricemia model, but not an MSU-induced acute-gout flare model.
