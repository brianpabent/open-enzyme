Date: 2026-05-20

Status: wiki-update candidate for open-access 2022/2023 animal-model evidence; 2025 CNKI comparator ambiguity remains unresolved.

## Why this artifact exists

The CNKI P0-1 rerun surfaced a 2025 *Journal of Jilin Agricultural University* record on *Sanghuangporus vaninii* alcohol extract, but the load-bearing CNKI abstract phrase says the middle/high-dose extract groups improved "compared with the allopurinol group." That comparator wording is biologically surprising and likely a machine-translation/source-rendering ambiguity. Local DOI resolution redirects to `www.chndoi.org`, which did not connect from this laptop, and CNKI `English HTML (MT)`, `HTML Reading`, `Original Reading`, PDF, and CAJ actions redirect to login/order flows. Therefore the 2025 CNKI record remains an abstract-level lead only.

The free-route search did, however, surface two open-access, source-readable *Sanghuangporus* papers that are directly relevant to OE's medicinal-mushroom complement track. These can be used for cautious wiki updates without relying on the unresolved 2025 CNKI comparator.

## Source 1 — Hua 2023, *Biomedicine & Pharmacotherapy*

- Title: `Sanghuangporus vaninii ethanol extract alleviates hyperuricemic renal injury by regulating the uric acid transporters and inhibiting HK-2 apoptosis`
- DOI: <https://doi.org/10.1016/j.biopha.2023.114970>
- Source page: <https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0753332223007606>
- Evidence level: Animal Model plus HK-2 cell model

Source-read facts:

- Acute-toxicity arm: ICR mice received single-gavage SHEE at 1,250, 2,500, and 5,000 mg/kg for 14 days. The paper reports LD50 above 5,000 mg/kg and no oral toxicity at or below 2,500 mg/kg.
- HUA renal-injury arm: ICR mice with potassium-oxonate + adenine-induced hyperuricemic kidney injury received SHEE at 125, 250, or 500 mg/kg.
- Readouts: serum UA, creatinine, BUN, XOD, liver ALT/AST, kidney histology, HK-2 proliferation/apoptosis under UA injury, and renal urate transporter expression.
- Directional result: SHEE reduced blood UA, Cr, BUN, and XOD, reduced liver ALT/AST, inhibited URAT1 and GLUT9 expression, promoted OAT1/OAT3/ABCG2 expression, and lowered apoptosis/caspase-3 activity.
- Interpretation: strong Animal Model support for *S. vaninii* ethanol extract as a renal urate-transporter / renal-injury lead. It is not human evidence and does not establish direct binding to transporters.

## Source 2 — Sun 2022, *Nutrients*

- Title: `Anti-Gouty Arthritis and Anti-Hyperuricemia Properties of Sanghuangporus vaninii and Inonotus hispidus in Rodent Models`
- DOI: <https://doi.org/10.3390/nu14204421>
- Source page: <https://www.mdpi.com/2072-6643/14/20/4421>
- Evidence level: Animal Model

Source-read facts:

- HUA mouse arm: 30 male BALB/c mice were randomized into five groups (`n = 6`): control, model, 20 mg/kg allopurinol, 1.5 g/kg *S. vaninii* extract, and 1.5 g/kg *I. hispidus* extract. Hyperuricemia used yeast extract powder plus potassium oxonate.
- Acute gouty arthritis rat arm: 30 male Wistar rats were randomized into five groups (`n = 6`): control, model, 0.3 mg/kg colchicine, 1.5 g/kg *S. vaninii*, and 1.5 g/kg *I. hispidus*. MSU was injected into the right ankle; swelling was tracked for 48 h.
- Directional HUA result: *S. vaninii* and *I. hispidus* reduced serum UA and serum/liver XOD in the YEP/OXO HUA model.
- Directional MSU-flare result: both extracts suppressed ankle swelling in MSU-induced acute gouty arthritis rats; histology and inflammatory-marker panels supported reduced inflammatory response.
- Interpretation: this is especially OE-relevant because it includes both HUA and MSU-flare animal models, not just chronic serum-urate lowering.

## Bottom Line

The Sanghuang branch is stronger than the CNKI abstract alone implied. The promotion-grade statement is: open-access animal-model evidence supports *S. vaninii* ethanol/whole extract as a HUA renal-injury lead through XOD and urate-transporter expression effects, and Sun 2022 adds a separate MSU-induced acute gouty arthritis rodent signal. The limitation is unchanged: no human efficacy evidence, and extract identity / dosing / batch reproducibility need SOP-level characterization before any OE product claim.
