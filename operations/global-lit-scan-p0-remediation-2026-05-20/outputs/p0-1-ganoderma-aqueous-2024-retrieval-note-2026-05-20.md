## Abstract-level facts only

Evidence tier if full text later confirms methods/results: **Animal Model plus In Vitro cell model**. Do not promote yet.

Visible abstract facts:

- Material: *Ganoderma lucidum* aqueous extract.
- Cell model: adenosine-induced hyperuricemic L-O2 cell model; extract tested at 0.25, 0.50, and 1.00 mg/mL.
- Animal model: potassium oxazinate plus yeast-extract-induced hyperuricemic rat model; extract gavage at 0.5, 1.0, and 2.0 g/kg.
- Reported positive signals: reduced uric acid content in L-O2 culture medium; medium/high doses decreased serum uric acid; serum/hepatic xanthine oxidase activity decreased; serum BUN and creatinine decreased; renal/hepatic nuclear-to-cytoplasmic ratio decreased in specified groups.
- Critical unresolved nuance: the abstract says kidney-tissue uric acid increased at all extract doses even while serum urate decreased. That could reflect altered renal handling, tissue deposition, assay/timing effects, or translation/abstract compression. It is load-bearing for any "renal protective" claim.
