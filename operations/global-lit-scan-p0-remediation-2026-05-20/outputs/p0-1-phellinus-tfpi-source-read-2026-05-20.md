Date: 2026-05-20

## Source

- Chen D, Jiang C, Lu H. `Study on the mechanism of Phellinus igniarius total flavonoids in reducing uric acid and protecting uric acid renal injury in vitro`. *Heliyon* 2023;9(1):e12979. DOI [`10.1016/j.heliyon.2023.e12979`](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.heliyon.2023.e12979). PMCID [`PMC9938467`](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC9938467/). PMID 36820194.

This is an English open-access source, so the two-model translation protocol is not triggered. It is relevant because the 2025 CNKI TFPI abstract recovered in P0-1 has an internally nuanced transporter mRNA/protein statement; this 2023 open source provides a promotion-grade TFPI anchor for HUA / uric-acid-nephropathy evidence while leaving the 2025 CNKI transporter mismatch unresolved.

## Extraction / identity

The source used *Phellinus igniarius* `Zhehuang No. 1` powder from Zhejiang Qiandao Lake Sangdu Edible Fungus Professional Cooperative. TFPI was prepared by 70% ethanol reflux extraction and AB-8 macroporous adsorption resin purification. The total flavonoid content was reported as 66.7%.

## Animal model

Evidence tier: **Animal Model**.

- Model: male ICR mice, potassium-oxonate-induced hyperuricemia / uric-acid-nephropathy model.
- Groups: control, model, allopurinol 10 mg/kg, TFPI 50/150/450 mg/kg; n=10 each.
- Day-7 induction: all non-control groups received 350 mg/kg potassium oxonate 0.5 h before final administration.

Interpretation: TFPI lowered serum urate versus the model group, with 150 and 450 mg/kg significant in the reported table. The 450 mg/kg dose also lowered serum creatinine versus model. Liver XOD decreased versus model across TFPI groups; ADA was not the primary positive signal.

## Cell model

Evidence tier: **In Vitro**.

- Model: HK-2 human proximal tubular epithelial cells.
- Injury: MSU 150 mg/L for 24 h.
- TFPI doses: 37.5, 75, 150 mg/L.
- Readouts: viability, LDH, apoptosis/pyroptosis, ROS, mitochondrial apoptosis markers, TLR4/NF-kB/TXNIP/NLRP3, IL-1beta/TNF-alpha, ABCG2 protein.

Source-read result: TFPI protected HK-2 cells from MSU-induced injury, reduced LDH release, apoptosis, ROS, caspase-3 / cleaved-caspase-3 / Bax signal, TLR4/NF-kB/p-NF-kB/TXNIP/NLRP3 proteins, and IL-1beta / TNF-alpha release. ABCG2 protein expression increased after TFPI treatment.

## Boundary conditions

- Do not promote this as human evidence.
- Do not promote this as synovial gout-flare evidence. The in vitro model is renal proximal tubular HK-2 cells, not synoviocytes or macrophages in an acute flare compartment.
- Do not use this to resolve the 2025 CNKI TFPI transporter mismatch. The 2025 record remains manual/full-text-needed for OAT1/ABCG2/URAT1 mRNA-versus-protein interpretation.
- The authors state limitations: animal models differ from humans, active components were not fully separated, and only total flavonoids were studied.

## Wiki-update decision

Promote *Phellinus igniarius* TFPI from "abstract-lead / traditional-name rescan" to a cautious **Animal Model + In Vitro renal-cell** source-read anchor for HUA / uric-acid-nephropathy. The appropriate mechanism language is XOD reduction plus renal-cell TLR4/NLRP3 suppression and ABCG2 upregulation, not direct URAT1 binding and not clinical efficacy.
