# Chaperone framework predictive-power validation

## Reflection

Is the chaperone-orthogonal stacking framework actually predictive, or is it sophisticated retrofit?

The 2026-05-06 walkthrough surfaced two material weakening corrections:
- Wakai 2019's "supra-additive 3-cellulase synergy" was actually copy-number + promoter engineering, not synergy
- Zhang 2006's "cross-class combinations 5× advantage" was cross-paper Yu 2017 comparison; intra-paper advantage is 1.2-1.5×

The framework's central strategic claim ("cross-class helper combinations are highest-leverage capacity-expansion lever") is materially weakened.

The triple-cassette prediction (architecture-adjusted 0.35-0.65) is the first prospective test the framework has made — not retrofit. The 2026-05-08 walkthrough Items 5 + 11 added explicit calibration-set framing: §1.9 (lactoferrin transferrin-lobe) + §1.25 (DAF SCR1-4 CCP/SCR) span the framework's α-coefficient extremes and would calibrate it under harmonized conditions ([`chaperone-orthogonal-stacking.md` §3.5.4](../../wiki/chaperone-orthogonal-stacking.md)).

## Trigger

When the §1.9 Lf-alone arm + dual-cassette arm + (if pursued) triple-cassette arm wet-lab data lands.

- Lf-alone titer >500 mg/L would shift the upper bound of the triple-cassette prediction more favorably
- Lf-alone titer <40 mg/L would confirm the lower bound
- If the predicted triple synergy 0.45-0.55 lands accurately within ±0.10 of measured, that's first real validation that the load-class scoring rules have cross-substrate predictive power
- If it lands >0.20 off, the framework needs revision — the qualitative prediction (PDI substrates compete) may stand, but the quantitative coefficients calibrated from non-koji data don't transfer

## Outcome

Decide whether to:

a. Keep using the framework as a prospective design tool
b. Restrict it to qualitative direction-only claims
c. Recalibrate coefficients against the new koji data
d. Deprecate the framework in favor of pcSecKoji-style proteome-constrained model
