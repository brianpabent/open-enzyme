2. **The mechanical-flare-triggers page identifies five candidate mechanisms for exertion-preceded flares, but only candidate #5 (metabolic-overload → impaired urate excretion) has a testable prediction at n=1 with existing infrastructure: post-exertion serum UA should rise if mechanism #5 dominates, and should be unchanged if mechanisms #1–4 dominate.** The Li XD 2012 data (fatigue/overwork 19.3% vs. trauma 0.35%) already favors mechanism #5 — the empirical asymmetry between these categories in the same primary cohort argues that whatever is happening with activity-preceded flares is more likely fatigue/metabolic-mediated than mechanical-impact-mediated. But the Li data is self-report, not kinetic measurement. The proposed exertion-challenge test (Connection 1 / Proposed Experiment 1 above) would be the first direct kinetic measurement of the post-exertion UA trajectory in a gout patient, and would resolve the dominant uncertainty in the mechanical-flare-triggers page with a single $50 experiment. The question is: should this experiment be added to `validation-experiments.md` as a new §3.11, or should it replace/supplement the existing §3.10 fructose challenge? Recommendation: **supplement** §3.10 rather than replace — the fructose challenge tests acute uricase efficacy (a different question), and the two probes together (fructose → UA spike via PRPS/XO pathway; exertion → UA spike via impaired renal clearance) would characterize Brian's urate-handling dynamics under two different challenge modalities, each informative about different mechanism axes. The exertion challenge additionally tests the mechanical-trigger hypothesis (if UA is unchanged but a flare occurs), which the fructose challenge cannot address.
