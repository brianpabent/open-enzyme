The single load-bearing belief in the current platform thesis that is *least* supported by the corpus is not the gut-lumen sink mechanism (H08, which has ALLN-346 Study 201 signal and comp-019 flux-model support, weak as those are). It is the **quantitative transferability of the chaperone-orthogonal-stacking framework's α coefficients to *A. oryzae* solid-state koji fermentation.** Every architecture decision downstream of the §1.9 dual-cassette feasibility test — whether DAF SCR1-4 routes to a separate strain or the endgame strain, whether triple-cassette designs are feasible, whether the single-strain endgame thesis holds, whether future secreted disulfide-rich payloads can be stacked — rests on α coefficients derived from non-koji in vitro folding kinetics (Notari 2023 for transferrin-lobe, Schmidt 2010 for CCP/SCR structural rigidity, Vinci 2004/Feige 2009 for Ig-like, all mammalian or yeast systems). The framework's own §8 item 6 explicitly flags the calibration uncertainty: "The framework's quantitative synergy coefficients are extrapolated from Pichia, A. niger, and yeast literature; they may need rescaling once direct koji data is generated." The §3.5.4 calibration set (§1.9 lactoferrin + §1.25 DAF SCR1-4 under harmonized NSlD-ΔP10 / solid-state conditions) is designed to close this gap, but it has not been executed. Until those data land, every α-dependent prediction is a bounded extrapolation, and the bounds are wide: Lf α = 1.5–2.5 (a 1.7× range), DAF α = 0.3–0.6 (a 2× range). The combined triple-cassette prediction spans 0.35–0.65 — encompassing both "augmentation-feasible" and "separate-strain routing" outcomes. The platform is designing against the midpoint of an uncalibrated range.
