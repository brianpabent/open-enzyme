# Does prolonged fasting in gout patients produce a clinically significant cortisol × BHB × UA interaction that modulates flare risk beyond what the ketone-urate competition alone predicts?

1. **Does prolonged fasting in gout patients produce a clinically significant cortisol × BHB × UA interaction that modulates flare risk beyond what the ketone-urate competition alone predicts?** The BHB active-flare contraindication is grounded in the transient UA spike from ketone-urate renal competition. But fasting also elevates cortisol, which has its own anti-inflammatory effects (the basis for prednisone's use in acute flare). The three-axis interaction is uncharacterized. This matters operationally: if cortisol's anti-inflammatory effect partially offsets the UA-spike risk, the "suspend fasting during flares" guidance might be overly conservative for some patients. *(Speculative; see Proposed Experiment #1 above.)*
