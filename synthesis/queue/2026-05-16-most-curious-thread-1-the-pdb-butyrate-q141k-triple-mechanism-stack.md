# The PDB-butyrate-Q141K triple-mechanism stack.

The PDB-butyrate-Q141K triple-mechanism stack. Of everything in this corpus, this is the thread I'd spend the next experiment slot on. The mechanism is the most elegant in the entire platform: gut bacteria (PDB) consume uric acid and produce butyrate as a metabolic byproduct; that butyrate is the exact HDAC inhibitor that rescues the Q141K ABCG2 trafficking defect (Basseville 2012, PMID 22472121); the rescued ABCG2 delivers more urate to the gut lumen; and gut-lumen uricase (engineered koji or endogenous residual) degrades it. This is a natural, endogenous, genotype-targeted therapy — the bacteria produce the molecule that fixes your genetic variant, and the only "intervention" is dietary fiber + selenium adequacy. The corpus evidence supporting this hunch: (a) PDB are depleted in gout patients and produce butyrate from urate (`purine-degrading-bacteria.md` §"SCFA Downstream Effects"), (b) butyrate rescues Q141K trafficking via HDAC inhibition at 1–5 mM in vitro (`abcg2-modulators.md` §6, Basseville 2012), (c) the gut-lumen sink mechanism is multiplicative on residual ABCG2 capacity (comp-019, `uricase-abcg2-genotype-stratification-computational.md`), and (d) Q141K-positive patients get a synergy bonus from rescue interventions because restored ABCG2 provides more substrate for the sink to amplify (`abcg2-modulators.md` §"Q141K trafficking rescue"). The evidence that would refute it: the Caco-2 butyrate dose-response experiment in `validation-experiments.md` §1.14. If endogenous colonic butyrate concentrations (after the mucus-layer + epithelial-surface gradient drop from luminal levels) cannot reach the HDAC-inhibitory threshold at the enterocyte nucleus, the endogenous-PDB thesis is mechanistically elegant but quantitatively irrelevant — and the platform needs engineered butyrate delivery (LBP chassis) to close the loop. The cheapest discriminating experiment: the §1.14 butyrate dose-response arm — WT + Q141K Caco-2, 5 concentrations bracketing 0.05–5 mM, dual readout (ABCG2 surface expression + functional urate efflux), ~$500–1,500 as an add-on to the existing factorial. This is the single experiment that converts the most elegant mechanism in the corpus from "plausible" to "quantitatively anchored" (or falsified). I suspect this is a convergent pick — the mechanism's elegance is independent of the synthesizer model, and the concentration-gap question is the obvious next step regardless of who's reading the corpus. But the specific framing of PDB as an endogenous gene-therapy-by-microbiome — bacteria producing the molecule that fixes your most common gout-risk variant — may be idiosyncratic to this reader's taste for mechanism stories that collapse multiple intervention layers into a single evolutionary narrative.
