# Does the "rigidify inter-domain linkers with proline substitutions" strategy generalize beyond lactoferrin to other secreted OE payloads with inter-domain linkers (DAF SCR1-4, future payloads)?

1. **Does the "rigidify inter-domain linkers with proline substitutions" strategy generalize beyond lactoferrin to other secreted OE payloads with inter-domain linkers (DAF SCR1-4, future payloads)?** The structural pattern — well-folded domains connected by protease-accessible linkers — appears in both Lf (inter-lobe linker, comp-005/comp-034) and DAF (Ser/Thr stalk, comp-006/comp-012). If the proline-substitution strategy validates for Lf in the comp-034 wet-lab follow-up, testing it on the DAF SCR1-4 inter-SCR linkers (the short loops connecting SCR1→SCR2, SCR2→SCR3, SCR3→SCR4) would establish whether this is a generalizable design rule or a protein-specific optimization. (context: `lactoferrin-linker-redesign-computational.md`, `daf-cd55-scr14-truncated-computational.md`)

> **Pass 3 review — Partial.** `[OVERLAP: EXTENSION]` `[GAP: science-gap]` The general design question is valid for future secreted payloads with exposed inter-domain linkers, but DAF SCR1–4 is the wrong exemplar. comp-012 says stalk truncation removes all exposed DAF sites and leaves the SCR1–4 core LOW risk; it does not identify the short inter-SCR linkers as a remaining protease-liability target. Reframe the open question as “does proline-rigidification generalize to exposed flexible linkers in future payloads,” not “test it on DAF SCR1–4 inter-SCR linkers.”
