# Should the medicinal-mushroom-complement track's candidate species table include ascomycete secondary-metabolite producers (Penicillium, Aspergillus spp. beyond A. oryzae, Monascus)?

3. **Should the medicinal-mushroom-complement track's candidate species table include ascomycete secondary-metabolite producers (Penicillium, Aspergillus spp. beyond A. oryzae, Monascus)?** The current candidate species table covers only basidiomycetes (Ganoderma, Cordyceps, Pleurotus, Lentinula, Hericium, Trametes, Inonotus). The comp-014 Phase 3 Berkeleyamide finding and the logic of Connection 1 above suggest that food-grade ascomycetes may access different secondary-metabolite chemistry space than basidiomycetes — including direct caspase-1 inhibitors that the basidiomycete corpus lacks. Monascus purpureus (red yeast rice, already GRAS, already a commercial supplement organism producing monacolins/lovastatin) and food-grade Penicillium species (cheese ripening) are the most natural first entries. The counterargument: ascomycete secondary-metabolite discovery rapidly converges on mycotoxin risk (aflatoxin, ochratoxin, patulin, citrinin — many Penicillium and Aspergillus species produce these); the basidiomycete medicinal mushrooms are mycotoxin-safe by comparison. The chassis-pending question is whether the chemistry-space advantage justifies the toxin-screening overhead.
