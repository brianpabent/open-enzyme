   - *Why It Matters:* `gout-genetic-variants.md` §Category 5 identifies CFH Y402H (rs1061170) as a complement-amplification variant — the risk allele reduces CFH's ability to bind CRP and host glycosaminoglycans, weakening inactivation of surface-deposited C3b. The predicted consequence: more vigorous alternative-pathway C5a generation on MSU crystals, producing more intense NLRP3 priming at CP0. This is the same stratification logic as ABCG2 Q141K × butyrate: a genetic variant produces a pathway-level vulnerability, and a specific intervention class (dietary CP0 candidates — rosmarinic acid at C3 convertase, luteolin at CH50/AP50, Houttuynia cordata multi-target) is mechanistically positioned to bypass that vulnerability. The dietary CP0 candidates operate **upstream of** CFH — rosmarinic acid covalently modifies C3b before CFH-dependent regulation matters; luteolin and Houttuynia block convertase assembly at a step CFH doesn't regulate. This means CFH Y402H carriers should in principle benefit MORE from dietary CP0 blockade than wild-type carriers — the same prediction the Q141K × butyrate thesis makes for ABCG2. The nearest clinical analog (CFH Y402H × dietary supplementation × AMD progression) points in the **opposite** direction — Vavvas 2018 found paradoxical worsening (HR 2.9, p=0.018) for CFH high-risk genotypes on AREDS zinc/antioxidant formulation, and Merle 2015 found CFH × DHA interaction p=0.01 with DHA protective only in non-risk genotypes. But the mechanism-dissociation hypothesis is plausible: AREDS zinc and DHA may work through CFH-dependent complement regulation (which CFH 402H performs poorly), while the comp-018 dietary candidates work via direct C3b modification / convertase inhibition (which bypasses CFH entirely). Whether the dissociation holds is empirically open — and the biobank data to test it exists (UK Biobank: CFH rs1061170 on the standard Axiom array, gout outcomes coded, Oxford WebQ dietary recall available, Phenol-Explorer-derived polyphenol intake published for UKB). The cross-tabulation (CFH genotype × dietary polyphenol intake × incident gout) is technically straightforward and has not been run. This is a $0 lit-scan-then-biobank-mining question — the same cost class as the Q141K × uricase genotype-stratification literature scan that comp-019 executed.
