# Anakinra SC accessibility audit — insurance coverage and rheumatologist willingness for off-label gout prescribing.

3. **Anakinra SC accessibility audit — insurance coverage and rheumatologist willingness for off-label gout prescribing.** *Cost: $0 (phone calls + literature review). Time: 1-2 weeks.* `[PHASE-A-MATCH: no]`

   - *What it tests:* The `gout-action-guide.md` anakinra protocol is detailed and mechanistically grounded, but the gap between "here's the protocol" and "a patient can actually get this prescribed" is uncharacterized. This experiment answers: (a) what's the actual insurance coverage rate for off-label anakinra in gout? (b) what's the rheumatologist willingness-to-prescribe rate? (c) what's the real-world patient experience (injection-site reactions, effectiveness at 3-day protocol)?
   - *Protocol:* (1) PubMed + Google Scholar search for "anakinra gout real-world" and "anakinra off-label prescribing patterns." (2) Reddit r/gout + r/ankylosingspondylitis + patient forums search for anakinra experiences. (3) Call 2-3 rheumatology practices (anonymized, information-gathering) to ask about off-label anakinra for gout. Compile findings into a pragmatic-accessibility addendum to `gout-action-guide.md`.
   - *Decides:* Whether the anakinra SC recommendation in the action guide is practically actionable for Brian's situation, or whether it's a mechanism-grounded recommendation with an uncharacterized access barrier. The cost of the experiment is zero; the value is converting "here's a protocol" into "and here's how to actually get it."
