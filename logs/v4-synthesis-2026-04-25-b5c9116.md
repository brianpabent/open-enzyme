## Contradictions Found

1. **The intracellular vs. secreted uricase strategy is a central, unresolved tension across the platform, with different documents implicitly favoring opposite approaches.**
   - *Locations:* `engineered-yeast-uricase-proposal.md` and `cross-validation.md` favor intracellular expression for its cell-wall protection during GI transit. `engineered-koji-protocol.md` defaults to a secreted strategy (Koji-S) to leverage *A. oryzae*'s high secretion capacity, while acknowledging the acid-survival problem. `koji-home-fermentation.md` reinforces the risk, noting that un-coated enzymes likely only provide pre-digestion value.
   - *Analysis:* The platform lacks a unified strategy for oral enzyme delivery. The yeast track prioritizes GI survival via encapsulation in the cell itself. The koji track prioritizes expression titer via secretion, leaving GI survival as a downstream formulation problem. The new `koji-home-fermentation.md` page strengthens the argument that free enzymes in a food matrix will not survive gastric transit, making the default Koji-S strategy in `engineered-koji-protocol.md` riskier than presented. This needs to be resolved before committing to a final construct, as it is a fundamental design choice.
   - *Suggested Action:* Formalize the SGF/SIF survival experiment (from `synthesis.md` 2026-04-25) as the gating test for this decision. Until then, revise `engineered-koji-protocol.md` to present intracellular (Koji-I) as the default, lower-risk strategy, with secretion (Koji-S) as a high-yield alternative requiring enteric coating.

   {{PEER-REVIEW}}
