   - *Analysis:* Both positions are internally consistent but they answer different questions. The chaperone framework uses Huynh 2020 as a *capacity* benchmark (how much ER folding load can the host handle?), while the endgame strain page uses Ward 1995 as a *titer* benchmark (what yield can this specific protein achieve?). The tension arises because the chaperone framework's synergy coefficients are calibrated against the Huynh 2020 capacity ceiling — if that ceiling is not the true limit for lactoferrin (because Lf is a single-chain protein, not a dual-chain antibody), then the predicted synergy coefficients may be systematically conservative. Conversely, if the Huynh 2020 ceiling is real for any PDI-heavy payload regardless of chain architecture, then the endgame strain's reliance on Ward 1995's submerged-culture titer may be overoptimistic for solid-state koji. This is not a resolvable contradiction without data, but it is a load-bearing ambiguity that both pages should acknowledge.
   - *Suggested Action:* Add a cross-reference note in both pages acknowledging the ambiguity: the chaperone framework's synergy predictions are calibrated against the Huynh 2020 capacity ceiling; if Ward 1995's >2 g/L Lf titer reflects a higher true capacity for single-chain glycoproteins, the synergy coefficients may be conservative. The §1.9 wet-lab experiment is the only way to resolve this.
