## Riskiest Assumption

The single load-bearing belief in the current platform thesis that is least supported by the corpus is **the assumption that the gut-lumen uricase sink can produce clinically meaningful serum urate reduction (≥1 mg/dL) in a typical gout population at the engineered-koji target dose of ~25 mg/day.** The cross-validation.md page rates the gut-lumen mechanism at 6.5/10 (upgraded from 6/10 post-comp-019 flux model) and explicitly states: "clinical magnitude prediction in the −0.5 to −1.0 mg/dL band awaits Phase 2b RCT verification." The comp-019 flux model predicts −0.83 mg/dL for WT/WT males at 25 mg/day (90% CI −1.13 to −0.57), which is below the 1-2 mg/dL therapeutic target range cited in cross-validation.md. The ALLN-346 Phase 2a Study 201 showed a signal in CKD patients but was terminated before a pivotal readout; Study 202 showed no advantage over placebo in a broader cohort. No oral uricase program has demonstrated a ≥1 mg/dL mean reduction in an unselected gout population. The entire platform thesis — engineered koji as a gut-lumen uricase delivery vehicle — rests on the assumption that the effect size is large enough to be clinically meaningful as an adjunct to allopurinol. If the true effect size is at the lower end of the flux model's CI (−0.57 mg/dL) or below, the platform's value proposition weakens substantially. This assumption is anchored to cross-validation.md Claim 1 and the comp-019 flux model predictions, with the absence of a confirmatory Phase 2b RCT being the specific evidence gap.
