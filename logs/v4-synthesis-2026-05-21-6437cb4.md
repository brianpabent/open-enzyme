## Contradictions Found

1. **The `Tier 2 butyrate assay gap` in `genotype-informed-supplement-workflow.md` and the `mechanical-flare-triggers` 劳累 mechanism create a compound uncertainty for Q141K carriers that the corpus has not named: a Q141K-positive patient who flares after exertion cannot distinguish "the butyrate intervention is working but the exertion trigger overwhelmed it" from "the butyrate intervention was under-dosed and the flare would have happened anyway."** Locations: `genotype-informed-supplement-workflow.md` §"Worked example — ABCG2 Q141K heterozygous carrier, butyrate-emphasis stack" steps 4–5; `mechanical-flare-triggers.md` §"Candidate mechanism #5 — metabolic overload via exertion/fatigue"; `tier-2-butyrate-assay-audit-computational.md` §"Verdict." Analysis: The workflow's Q141K worked example instructs the user to track serum UA quarterly + the standard four-biomarker panel, with the prediction from comp-019 that Q141K heterozygotes may see a smaller ΔSUA than WT/WT under gut-lumen uricase but may gain a synergy bonus from butyrate-mediated HDAC trafficking rescue. The mechanical-flare-triggers page establishes that 劳累 (fatigue/overwork) at 19.3% is a dominant non-dietary flare trigger, with candidate mechanism #5 (metabolic overload → impaired urate excretion → transient hyperuricemia → crystal nucleation or supersaturation) empirically favored over pure mechanical shedding. These two threads create a compound uncertainty for a Q141K-positive patient who is tracking flares: a post-exertion flare could be (a) a butyrate-intervention failure (the trafficking rescue didn't achieve sufficient ABCG2 surface expression to handle the exertion-driven urate load), (b) a butyrate-dose failure (the supplement was under-dosed but the patient can't verify because no Tier 2 butyrate-potency assay exists per comp-038), (c) a mechanism-independence problem (exertion triggers flares via mechanical shedding, not metabolic overload, and butyrate's ABCG2 rescue is irrelevant to that mechanism), or (d) regression to the mean (the patient would have flared anyway). The four explanations are not distinguishable without either (i) a Tier 2 butyrate-potency assay (closes option b), (ii) an exertion-challenge test with UA monitoring (discriminates option a from options c/d — per Connection 1 above), or (iii) a genotype-stratified n-of-many cohort (beyond n=1 resolution). The corpus currently has none of these. This is not a contradiction in mechanism claims — both mechanism threads can be simultaneously true — but it is a contradiction in *operational resolvability at n=1*, and the workflow page's implicit promise is n=1 resolvability. The compound uncertainty should be named so the user understands which interventions have verified dose tracking and which don't, and what kinds of flare events can and cannot be attributed to intervention performance.

## Riskiest Assumption

The single load-bearing belief in the current platform thesis that is least supported by the corpus is the **genotype-informed-supplement-workflow's implicit claim that the five-step closed-loop pipeline (genotype → compound selection → produce/source → Tier 2 batch QC → calibrated dose → biomarker tracking) is n=1-operational for every intervention class the platform recommends.** The workflow has been instantiated at n=1 for zero interventions — it exists as a documented specification, not as a field-tested protocol. The Q141K butyrate worked example is the only worked example; it documents a Tier 2 assay gap (comp-038 YELLOW — no home colorimetric butyrate assay exists) that breaks the "calibrate once at Tier 3, track batches at Tier 2" discipline at step 4 for the very intervention the worked example prioritizes. The cordycepin Tier 2 diazo-coupling assay is speculative (§1.28, not yet validated). The EGT Ellman's reagent Tier 2 assay is well-established but no multi-operator reproducibility data exists. The GLPP Tier 2 phenol-sulfuric assay measures total polysaccharide, not GLPP specifically. Every Tier 2 assay in the pipeline has a documented gap or caveat; none has been validated in a multi-user setting. The workflow's five steps are individually specified but have never been executed end-to-end by any user, including Brian. H09 (Community Fermentation Reliability) is the platform-level test of the production side; no equivalent falsification card exists for the *workflow integration* side — the claim that a user can genotype themselves, select a compound, produce or source it, verify the batch potency at Tier 2, calibrate the dose, and track biomarkers in a closed loop. The workflow is specified; the Tier 2 assays are specified; the Tier 3 anchors are specified; the self-experiment protocol is specified. Every component exists. No one has run the full loop. The single most informative near-term experiment is not a new wet-lab gate — it's a **dry run of the workflow end-to-end on one compound where all Tier 2 infrastructure is already validated** (ergothioneine — Ellman's reagent is well-established, *P. citrinopileatus* cultivation is home-feasible, Tier 3 HILIC-HPLC anchor exists, OCTN1 variant is genotypable via clinical PCR). Cost: ~$500 (genotyping + grow kit + Ellman's reagent + one Tier 3 HPLC anchor). Time: 8–12 weeks (cultivation cycle + assay + biomarker tracking). This is a faster path to surfacing workflow failures than waiting for the §1.9 Ward 1995 dual-cassette strain to exist.

*Anchor: `genotype-informed-supplement-workflow.md` §"Why this exists" claims the workflow blocks two failure modes (silent underdosing and genotype-blind selection) and §"How this fits with H09" claims the workflow survives even if H09 fails. The Q141K worked example's step 4 uses stool SCFA as a Tier 2 proxy that comp-038 explicitly says is not a direct potency measurement. `quantification-ladder.md` §"The operational pattern" requires "calibrate once at Tier 3, track batches at Tier 2" — and for butyrate, Tier 2 tracking does not exist. The workflow's design is sound; its field-readiness for butyrate specifically (the intervention its only worked example uses) has not been verified.*

## Most Curious Thread

Of everything in this corpus, the single thread I'd spend the next experiment slot on is the **CFH Y402H × dietary-CP0 intersection.** The mechanistic case is clean: CFH Y402H reduces Factor H's ability to regulate alternative-pathway complement → more C5a generated on MSU crystals → more NLRP3 priming at CP0 → more intense flares. The dietary CP0 candidates (rosmarinic acid, luteolin, Houttuynia cordata) operate upstream of CFH — they block C3 convertase assembly at a step Factor H doesn't regulate — so CFH Y402H carriers should in principle benefit MORE from dietary CP0 blockade than wild-type carriers. This is the same stratification logic as Q141K × butyrate, the most-developed genotype-stratified intervention in the entire OE corpus. (a) *Corpus evidence supporting:* `gout-genetic-variants.md` §Category 5 documents CFH Y402H with allele frequency (~36–39% European, ~35–37% African, ~30% South Asian, ~5–6% East Asian), complement-amplification mechanism, and elevated CRP in 402HH carriers (Hecker 2023, PMID 37940657). `complement-c5a-gout.md` §2.6 establishes classical pathway as the dominant initiator of MSU complement activation — the same pathway whose amplification CFH regulates. `upstream-complement-modulator-sweep-computational.md` documents rosmarinic acid, luteolin, and Houttuynia as dietary CP0 candidates with multi-anchor evidence. (b) *Evidence that would refute it:* The CFH × diet × AMD literature points the opposite direction — Vavvas 2018 found paradoxical worsening (HR 2.9) for CFH high-risk on AREDS, and Merle 2015 found DHA protective only in CFH non-risk genotypes — suggesting the generalization "CFH variant carriers benefit from supplementation that targets the same pathway" may be naive. A null or inverted CFH × dietary-polyphenol × gout association in UK Biobank would refute the prediction and establish that the AMD pattern generalizes to gout. (c) *Cheapest discriminating experiment:* A $0 biobank-mining feasibility assessment (Proposed Experiment 2 above) followed by collaboration with an existing gout-GWAS group (Merriman/Otago, Major-Wrigley/Auckland, Choi/MGH) for the actual cross-tabulation — ~3-month timeline, $0 OE cost if a collaborator shares UKB access. This is the same cost class as comp-019's Phase A literature mining that produced the finding "zero Q141K-stratified uricase trials exist" — and the information yield could be comparably large. Multi-vendor signal: I suspect another sweep model would also converge on this thread because (i) the Q141K × butyrate pattern is the platform's canonical genotype-stratified thesis and any structurally analogous pattern is high-value, (ii) the CFH entry in `gout-genetic-variants.md` was just added/refined and is top-of-mind, (iii) the dietary CP0 candidates are the newest Tier 1 findings from comp-018 Phase 2, and a model reading the corpus fresh would naturally ask "do these compose?" The convergence may be less idiosyncratic than typical — the pattern is already named for Q141K; CFH Y402H is the unnamed instance.
