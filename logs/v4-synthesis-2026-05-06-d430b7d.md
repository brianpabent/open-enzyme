   - *Why It Matters:* The chaperone-orthogonal stacking framework establishes that multi-cassette protein expression in *A. oryzae* is bottlenecked by competition for ER folding machinery (PDI, BiP, calnexin). Co-administering engineered koji (producing uricase, lactoferrin, DAF SCR1-4) with medicinal mushroom extracts can cover additional chokepoints — URAT1 via cordycepin, ADA via GLPP, redox via ergothioneine — without increasing the chaperone burden on the koji strain. ## Contradictions Found

1. **Ergothioneine titer contradiction: koji (20 mg/g) vs. medicinal mushroom claim of highest producer (7.0 mg/g).** Locations: `aspergillus-oryzae.md` (native secondary metabolites section), `medicinal-mushroom-complement-track.md` (candidate species table and Phase 7-1c findings). Analysis: The koji page cites ~20 mg/g dry mycelial mass for ergothioneine, while the medicinal mushroom page states *P. citrinopileatus* is the highest fungal producer at 7.0 mg/g DW. These numbers cannot both be correct if they refer to comparable dry-weight metrics. The koji number may be for optimized submerged mycelium, while the mushroom number is for fruiting body; or the koji number may be from a specific engineered strain. Resolution requires primary-source verification of both claims. If koji indeed produces 20 mg/g, the medicinal mushroom track's ergothioneine rationale is significantly weakened and the "highest producer" claim in the medicinal mushroom page is false.
