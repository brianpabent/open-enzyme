## Contradictions Found

1. **The chaperone‑orthogonal stacking framework claims that lactoferrin’s transferrin‑lobe architecture imposes an effective PDI load 1.5–2.5× its bulk disulfide count — but the foundational Notari 2023 kinetics were measured from a fully denatured state in vitro, and in the functional ER secretory pathway, co‑translational PDI engagement is likely faster. This could over‑estimate lactoferrin’s chaperone burden by a factor of 2–3×, deflating the triple‑cassette synergy prediction.** *Locations:* `wiki/chaperone-orthogonal-stacking.md` §3.5, §5.5; `wiki/c-utilis-uricase-cassette-compatibility-computational.md` §4.4. *Analysis:* The architecture coefficient (α) for the transferrin‑lobe fold was derived from Notari 2023’s in vitro oxidative refolding data, but the framework’s own caveat acknowledges that “in vivo ER‑assisted co‑translational folding with PDI acting on the nascent chain may be substantially faster.” If the true in‑vivo α for lactoferrin is closer to 1.0 (the default Ig‑like reference), then lactoferrin’s effective PDI load falls from 24–40 to 16, and the Lf × DAF SCR1‑4 combined effective load drops from 1.65–2.80× Huynh to 1.25–1.50× Huynh — a range that could shift the triple‑cassette synergy prediction from “separate‑strain routing” to “augmentation‑feasible.” This is not an error, but a **methodological tension** that the framework itself names but does not resolve. Until the §1.9 Lf‑alone arm provides an empirical PDI capacity anchor, the architecture‑adjusted synergy coefficients carry an unknowable downward bias.

   {{PEER-REVIEW}}

2. **The medicinal‑mushroom‑complement track’s Phase 6 triage committee routes GLPP to the “cultivation” track, but the comp‑013 TCM compound triage found that astilbin — the dominant bioactive from Smilax glabra — is GUT‑LUMINAL VIABLE and suppresses URAT1, XO, and NLRP3 simultaneously. Smilax glabra is a classical primary gout herb, and its non‑GLPP mechanism is transferable to a koji co‑fermentation or co‑delivery strategy, whereas GLPP’s large polysaccharide‑peptide complexes may be incompatible with koji’s secretory machinery.** *Locations:* `wiki/medicinal-mushroom-complement-track.md`, `wiki/tcm-gout-compound-triage-computational.md`. *Analysis:* comp‑013’s astilbin finding opens a separate entry point for a TCM‑derived herbal adjunct that could be administered alongside engineered koji or even integrated into a koji‑friendly fermented format (e.g., an amazake‑style product incorporating Smilax glabra decoction). This is a more platform‑synergistic route than whole‑mushroom cultivation, which requires separate fruiting‑body production. The two tracks (medicinal mushroom cultivation and TCM‑derived compound co‑delivery) are currently organised as distinct head‑level entities, but they **partially overlap** in their bioactive chemistries (e.g., ergothioneine appears in both, cordycepin spans both via the koji engineering route). The platform would benefit from a unified “fungal/herbal adjunct” exploration surface that cross‑indexes the top candidates by cultivation complexity, koji‑compatibility, and chokepoint coverage, rather than treating them as non‑overlapping silos.
