### 1. Tongkat ali (eurycomanone) + pharmacological chaperones as a two-layer Q141K rescue strategy — transcriptional upregulation + post-translational folding rescue.

*Speculative.* `[CHAIN-DEPTH: 3+]` `[PHASE-A-MATCH: no]`

- *Documents Connected:* `androgen-natural-modulation.md`, `abcg2-modulators.md`, `abcg2-q141k-chaperone-screen-computational.md`, `gout-genetic-variants.md`, `t-axis-adjuvant-urate-mapping-computational.md`
- *Page-pair linkage:* **Weakly connected.** `androgen-natural-modulation.md` cross-references `abcg2-modulators.md` but only in the context of ABCG2 inhibitor counter-indications (§"Gout-specific UA risk"). Neither page references the other's Q141K mechanism. `abcg2-q141k-chaperone-screen-computational.md` (comp-032) is not referenced by `androgen-natural-modulation.md` at all. The pharmacological-chaperone route (§"Pharmacological-chaperone route" in `abcg2-modulators.md`) and the tongkat ali multi-target transporter modulation (§1.7 in `androgen-natural-modulation.md`) have never been composed into a single intervention strategy.
- *Why It Matters:* Q141K is the #1 gout-risk GWAS variant. Two mechanistically orthogonal rescue routes exist in the corpus but have never been named as a combined strategy: **(a) Transcriptional upregulation** — eurycomanone from tongkat ali upregulates ABCG2, URAT1↓, GLUT9↓, NPT1↑ (PMID 31920654, Animal Model + In Vitro) and the 2021 Physta RCT shows SUA ↓7–11% in humans (n=105, Clinical Trial). **(b) Post-translational folding rescue** — pharmacological chaperones (diflunisal Tier 1, UDCA Tier 1, tafamidis Tier 2 per comp-032 GREEN, in silico) bind misfolded Q141K ABCG2 and restore trafficking from the ER aggresome to the apical membrane. These two mechanisms operate at different biological timescales (transcriptional = hours-to-days; chaperone = minutes-to-hours) and different molecular targets (promoter/enhancer vs. protein folding pocket). They should be **additive, not redundant.** A Q141K homozygote taking tongkat ali + low-dose diflunisal would get ABCG2 upregulation on any residual wild-type-like folding PLUS direct folding rescue of the Q141K variant — two independent pushes toward the same functional endpoint (more ABCG2 at the apical membrane). This is a genuinely novel composition across two corpus threads that have never cited each other. The 2021 Physta RCT did not stratify by Q141K genotype; comp-032's chaperone candidates have not been tested in combination with any transcriptional ABCG2 modulator.
- *Suggested Action:* Propose a Caco-2 Q141K transwell experiment: four arms (vehicle, eurycomanone 10 µM alone, diflunisal 10 µM alone, eurycomanone + diflunisal combination), 48h treatment, dual readouts (ABCG2 apical-membrane surface expression by Western + functional urate efflux by basolateral-to-apical flux). Cost ~$3–5K, 4–6 weeks. A positive combination result (combination > either alone) would justify a follow-on Q141K-stratified human pilot combining tongkat ali Physta + compounded low-dose diflunisal. Both interventions are already individually available (supplement + compounding pharmacy), so the translational path is shorter than for most OE chassis-pending entries. This experiment extends §1.14's Caco-2 infrastructure (already designed for Q141K transfection + butyrate dose-response) — marginal cost is adding two new treatment arms to an existing experimental framework.

### 2. Theaflavins as a genotype-informed renal-compensation lever for Q141K carriers — preferential weighting when intestinal ABCG2 is compromised.

*Supported.* `[CHAIN-DEPTH: 2]` `[PHASE-A-MATCH: no]`

- *Documents Connected:* `theaflavins.md`, `abcg2-modulators.md`, `gout-genetic-variants.md`, `genotype-informed-supplement-workflow.md`, `gout-pathophysiology.md`
- *Page-pair linkage:* **Weakly connected.** `theaflavins.md` is a relatively new page (2026-05-05) and has no cross-reference to `abcg2-modulators.md` or `gout-genetic-variants.md`. Its cross-references point to `egcg.md`, `nlrp3-inhibitor-screen.md`, `supplements-stack.md`, and `tnfsf14-gout-target.md`. The genotype-informed-supplement-workflow.md page has a Q141K worked example using butyrate — theaflavins are not mentioned as an alternative or complementary lever.
- *Why It Matters:* Q141K carriers have compromised intestinal ABCG2 — the gut urate secretion gate is partially closed regardless of transcriptional induction. Theaflavins have a unique multi-transporter renal urate handling profile documented in `theaflavins.md`: **↓URAT1, ↓GLUT9 (reabsorption block) + ↑OAT1, ↑OCTN1, ↑OAT2 (secretion enhancement).** This is the only compound in the OE supplement stack with documented simultaneous downregulation of both apical and basolateral urate reabsorption transporters PLUS upregulation of secretion transporters — and it operates at the **renal** compartment, which is the compensatory mechanism when **intestinal** ABCG2 is compromised. The logic: if the gut gate is partially closed (Q141K), open the kidney gate wider. Theaflavins do exactly this via a mechanism that doesn't depend on ABCG2 at all — it's URAT1/GLUT9/OAT-mediated. This is a genotype-informed supplement selection that the current `genotype-informed-supplement-workflow.md` Q141K worked example misses entirely: the worked example emphasizes butyrate (PPARγ → ABCG2 induction + HDAC → Q141K rescue), which targets the compromised transporter directly. Theaflavins target the **compensatory** transporter — a different strategy for the same genotype. The two are not mutually exclusive (butyrate + theaflavins could be combined), but the logic of *why* a Q141K carrier would preferentially weight theaflavins over, say, another NF-κB inhibitor has not been stated anywhere in the corpus.
- *Suggested Action:* Add a "Q141K-positive path → theaflavins" entry to `gout-action-guide.md` and `genotype-informed-supplement-workflow.md`, with the renal-compensation logic explicit. The theaflavins Tier 2 supplement entry in `supplements-stack.md` should carry a genotype-stratification note: "particularly relevant for Q141K carriers where intestinal ABCG2 is compromised — theaflavins enhance the renal excretion arm as compensation." This is a zero-cost documentation update with direct clinical-relevance implications for the platform's personalized-medicine thesis.
