## New Connections

1. **The medicinal mushroom complement track and the koji endgame strain are chaperone-orthogonal by design — mushroom compounds (polysaccharides, nucleosides, terpenoids) are off the protein secretion axis entirely, so they can be co-produced or co-administered without competing for ER folding capacity.** *Supported*. `[CHAIN-DEPTH: 2]` `[PHASE-A-MATCH: partial]`
   - *Documents Connected:* `medicinal-mushroom-complement-track.md`, `chaperone-orthogonal-stacking.md`, `koji-endgame-strain.md`, `modality-chokepoint-matrix.md`
   - *Page-pair linkage:* The medicinal mushroom complement track is newly added (Phase 7) and has no cross-references to the chaperone framework. The chaperone framework explicitly scores only protein payloads. The connection that mushroom compounds are “off the machinery axis” is novel, though the general principle of cytosolic/small-molecule payloads being orthogonal is already in the framework.
   - *Why It Matters:* The chaperone-orthogonal stacking framework currently focuses on protein secretion competition. The medicinal mushroom track introduces a class of payloads — polysaccharide-peptides (GLPP), nucleoside analogs (cordycepin), terpenoids, and thiol antioxidants (ergothioneine) — that are either cytosolic, small-molecule, or polysaccharide in nature, and thus do not load BiP, PDI, or calnexin. This means the entire medicinal mushroom complement can be added to the koji endgame strain’s output without affecting the predicted synergy coefficients for the protein payloads. In practice, a single consumer product could combine engineered koji (uricase + lactoferrin + DAF SCR1-4) with cultivated mushroom extracts (GLPP + cordycepin + ergothioneine) without any chaperone-competition penalty. This reframes the platform from “pick the best chassis” to “combine orthogonal chassis outputs.”
   - *Suggested Action:* Add a new row to the chaperone-orthogonal stacking framework’s candidate cassette table explicitly scoring the medicinal mushroom compound classes as “off-axis” (effective PDI load = 0, calnexin load = 0, BiP load = 0). This makes the orthogonality visible and provides a quantitative justification for combining the tracks.

The principle itself was codified from the *C. utilis* vs. *A. flavus* uricase decision (comp-011) and has not yet been applied to non-protein production decisions.
   - *Why It Matters:* The cordycepin production landscape is split: *C. militaris* GYS60 achieves 7,883 mg/L via submerged fermentation, while Jeennor 2023 demonstrated 564 mg/L/d in engineered *A. oryzae* via cns1+cns2.
