## Riskiest Assumption

The single load-bearing belief in the current platform thesis that is least supported by the corpus is **the chaperone-orthogonal stacking framework's per-architecture α coefficients as predictive of multi-cassette PDI/ERO1 competition in *A. oryzae* solid-state koji.** The framework drives critical platform-level decisions: DAF SCR1-4 is routed to separate-strain or LBP-chassis based on the triple-cassette prediction (0.35–0.65 synergy, below the 0.6 decision gate); the cytosolic-third-cassette design rule (§5.6) is derived from the framework's prediction that secreted triples saturate PDI capacity; the entire endgame-strain single-strain thesis rests on the prediction that uricase + Lf is within NSlD-ΔP10 capacity (architecture-adjusted effective load 24–40 vs Huynh reference 16.0). Yet the α coefficients are derived from non-koji in vitro folding kinetics and structural-rigidity arguments (Notari 2023 for transferrin-lobe, Schmidt 2010 for CCP/SCR, Vinci 2004/Feige 2009/Hellman 1999 for Ig-like), not from directly measured PDI residence times in *A. oryzae* or any filamentous fungus. The framework's own caveat (§8 item 6) explicitly names this as the central calibration uncertainty: "Notari 2023's lactoferrin oxidative folding kinetics were measured from a fully denatured state in vitro; in vivo ER-assisted co-translational folding with PDI acting on the nascent chain may be substantially faster, implying the transferrin-lobe α could be closer to 1.0 than 2.5 in the functional secretory context."

If that optimistic scenario holds (α ≈ 1.0 for in vivo ER-assisted folding), the triple-cassette prediction shifts upward toward the augmentation-feasible gate, the separate-strain routing of DAF SCR1-4 may be unnecessary, and the framework's decision-gate discipline has been systematically conservative — routing payloads to separate strains that could have been stacked. Conversely, if α is at the upper end (2.0–2.5 for Lf), the framework is correctly identifying a real capacity ceiling, and the separate-strain routing is the right call.

The calibration set (§1.9 + §1.25 under harmonized NSlD-ΔP10 / solid-state / matching-promoter conditions) is designed to resolve this, but as Contradiction 1 above documents, the §1.25 protocol-design gap currently threatens to forfeit the calibration data. **Until the calibration set returns empirical data, every decision the framework drives — including the single-strain vs separate-strain routing that gates the endgame strain's architecture — rests on α coefficients that have not been validated in the target host organism.**

See [`chaperone-orthogonal-stacking.md` §8 item 6](./chaperone-orthogonal-stacking.md) for the framework's own caveat; §3.5.4 for the calibration-set design; §5.5 for the triple-cassette prediction the α coefficients drive.
