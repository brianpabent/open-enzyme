1. **Native quaternary structure**  
   Homotetramer of four identical subunits. Each subunit has a molecular mass of approximately 34 kDa (total ~136 kDa).  
   *Evidence: X‑ray crystallography (in vitro).*  
   *Source: Colloc’h N et al. (1997) Nat Struct Biol 4:947–952.*

2. **Metal or organic cofactor**  
   No metal or organic cofactor is required. The enzyme is a cofactorless oxidase.  
   *Evidence: Crystal structure and biochemical characterization (in vitro).*  
   *Source: Colloc’h N et al. (1997) Nat Struct Biol 4:947–952.*

3. **Full balanced reaction catalyzed**  
   Uric acid + O₂ + H₂O → 5‑hydroxyisourate + H₂O₂  
   (5‑Hydroxyisourate then decays non‑enzymatically to allantoin + CO₂; the enzyme‑catalyzed step produces 5‑hydroxyisourate, not allantoin directly.)  
   *Evidence: Spectroscopic identification of the true enzymatic product (in vitro).*  
   *Source: Kahn K & Tipton PA (1997) J Am Chem Soc 119:5435–5442.*

4. **Peroxisomal targeting signal sequence and position**  
   Type 1 peroxisomal targeting signal (PTS1): **Ser‑Lys‑Leu (SKL)** at the extreme C‑terminus. In the deduced 302‑amino‑acid sequence, this corresponds to residues **300–302**.  
   *Evidence: Gene cloning and sequence analysis; functional PTS1 in fungi (in vitro/sequence).*  
   *Source: Legoux R et al. (1992) J Biol Chem 267:8565–8570.*

5. **Published Kₘ for uric acid**  
   **Kₘ = 5.1 µM** (uric acid).  
   *Evidence: Steady‑state kinetics of recombinant enzyme (in vitro).*  
   *Source: Legoux R et al. (1992) J Biol Chem 267:8565–8570.*
