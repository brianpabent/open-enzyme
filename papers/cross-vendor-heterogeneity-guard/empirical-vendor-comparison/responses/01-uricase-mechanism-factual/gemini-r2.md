Based on published data for *Aspergillus flavus* uricase:

1.  **Native Quaternary Structure**: The enzyme is a homotetramer. Each subunit has a molecular weight of approximately 34 kDa, forming a complete tetramer of ~136 kDa. (Evidence: in vitro, X-ray crystallography, size-exclusion chromatography).
    *   *Citation*: Colloc'h, N. et al. (1997). The 1.9-Å structure of the homotetrameric urate oxidase from *Aspergillus flavus*. *Nature Structural Biology*, 4(11), 947-952.

2.  **Cofactor Requirement**: Urate oxidase is a rare example of an oxidase that does not require a metal ion or an organic redox cofactor. The substrate (urate) directly reduces molecular oxygen within the active site. (Evidence: in vitro, X-ray crystallography and mechanistic studies).
    *   *Citation*: Gabison, L. et al. (2008). The crystal structure of a new form of urate oxidase from *Aspergillus flavus*. *Acta Crystallographica Section F*, 64(10), 884-888.

3.  **Balanced Reaction**: The enzyme catalyzes the initial oxidation of uric acid, which is followed by spontaneous hydrolysis and decarboxylation. The overall balanced reaction is:
    Uric acid + O₂ + H₂O → Allantoin + CO₂ + H₂O₂
    (Evidence: mechanistic extrapolation, in vitro).

4.  **Peroxisomal Targeting Signal (PTS)**: The native protein contains a canonical C-terminal peroxisomal targeting signal type 1 (PTS1).
    *   **Sequence**: -Ser-Lys-Leu-COOH (-SKL)
    *   **Position**: C-terminal tripeptide (residues 302-304 of the full-length 304-amino acid precursor).
    (Evidence: in vitro, protein sequence analysis).
    *   *Citation*: UniProtKB - P09949 (UOX_ASPFV).

5.  **Km for Uric Acid**: A published Km value for the recombinant *A. flavus* enzyme is **11.5 µM**. (Evidence: in vitro, enzyme kinetics).
    *   *Citation*: Kahn, K. et al. (1997). Urate Oxidase (Uricase): Probing the Mechanism of Action of a “Substrate-Activated” Enzyme. *Journal of Biological Chemistry*, 272(43), 27366-27372.
