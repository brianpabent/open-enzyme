For *Aspergillus flavus* uricase / urate oxidase (UOX; EC 1.7.3.3):

1. **Native quaternary structure** — **homotetramer**, ~**135 kDa** total; subunits ~**34 kDa** each, ~301 aa. Evidence: structural/in vitro.  
   *Primary sources:* Legoux et al., *J. Biol. Chem.* 1992, 267:8565–8570; Colloc’h et al., *Nat. Struct. Biol.* 1997, 4:947–952.

2. **Cofactor requirement** — **No metal ion or organic cofactor is required**; urate oxidases are cofactor-independent oxidases using O₂ directly. Evidence: biochemical/structural; mechanistic extrapolation across urate oxidases.  
   *Primary source:* Colloc’h et al., *Nat. Struct. Biol.* 1997, 4:947–952.

3. **Balanced reaction** — The enzymatic product is **5-hydroxyisourate (HIU)** plus hydrogen peroxide:  
   **urate + O₂ + H₂O + H⁺ → 5-hydroxyisourate + H₂O₂**  
   Often reported as the overall nonenzymatic downstream conversion to allantoin:  
   **uric acid + O₂ + 2 H₂O → allantoin + CO₂ + H₂O₂**  
   Evidence: in vitro enzymology; HIU identified as the true urate oxidase product.  
   *Primary source:* Kahn, Serfozo & Tipton, *J. Am. Chem. Soc.* 1997, 119:5435–5442.

   *Primary source:* Legoux et al., *J. Biol. Chem.* 1992, 267:8565–8570.
