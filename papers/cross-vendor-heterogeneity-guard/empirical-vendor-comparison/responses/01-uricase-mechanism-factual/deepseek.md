1. **Native quaternary structure**  
   Homotetramer; each subunit is ~34 kDa (301–302 residues), giving a total mass of ~135–140 kDa.  
   *Primary source*: Colloc’h E, et al. Crystal structure of the protein–uric acid complex at 2.05 Å resolution. *Nat Struct Biol* 4, 947–952 (1997). (PDB: 1UOX)

2. **Metal or organic cofactor**  
   No metal or organic cofactor is required – it is a cofactor‑less oxidase that activates O₂ directly.  
   *Evidence*: The 2.05 Å crystal structure shows no bound metal or prosthetic group (Colloc’h et al., 1997).

3. **Full balanced reaction**  
   Uric acid + O₂ + H₂O → 5‑hydroxyisourate + H₂O₂  
   (The immediate product 5‑hydroxyisourate then decarboxylates non‑enzymatically to allantoin + CO₂; this second step is not catalysed by the enzyme.)  
   *EC nomenclature*: EC 1.7.3.3, urate:oxygen oxidoreductase.

4. **Peroxisomal targeting signal**  
   A C‑terminal type‑1 peroxisomal targeting signal (PTS1), Ser‑Arg‑Leu (SRL), situated at the extreme C‑terminus (residues 300–302 in the 302‑amino‑acid native protein).  
   *Primary source*: Legoux R, et al. Cloning and expression in *Escherichia coli* of the gene encoding *Aspergillus flavus* urate oxidase. *J Biol Chem* 267, 8565–8570 (1992). (C‑terminal SRL confirmed from the deduced full‑length sequence.)

5. **Km for uric acid**  
   Reported as ~35 µM (3.5 × 10⁻⁵ M) for the native enzyme.  
   *Primary source*: Laboureur P, Langlois C. Purification et propriétés de l’urate‑oxydase d’*Aspergillus flavus*. *Bull Soc Chim Biol* 50, 811–825 (1968).  
   (Note: later studies on recombinant enzyme give similar values in the 30–60 µM range, depending on buffer conditions.)
