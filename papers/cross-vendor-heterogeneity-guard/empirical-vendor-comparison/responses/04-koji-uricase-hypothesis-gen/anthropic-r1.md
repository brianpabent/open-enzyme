# De-risking the *A. oryzae*::*A. flavus* uricase cassette — three Phase-0 experiments

**Stated assumptions**
- Native *A. flavus* uricase (UniProt Q12553 / *uaZ*) is cytosolic and peroxisomally targeted in its native host via a C-terminal PTS1 (-SKL or variant). Heterologous secretion of this enzyme has no strong precedent.
- "Food-grade" implies the final cassette must be markerless or use a food-compatible selection; that constraint is downstream and not addressed here.
- Target site of action is the small intestinal / colonic lumen (pH ~6–7.5, proteolytic, with bile salts). Gastric survival is presumed handled by formulation, not the protein.
- Cassette as drafted: P*amyB* — α-amylase SP — codon-optimized *uaZ* CDS — native terminator (unspecified).
- "Low-cost" assumes existing access to standard molecular biology reagents, a plate reader, and *E. coli* cloning capacity.

---

## Ranked by information-per-dollar

### 1. In silico secretion-competence and liability audit of the fusion ORF
**Cost: ~$0 · Timeline: 3–5 days**

**Question:** Does the proposed SP–uricase fusion have a predicted cleavable signal, and does the mature protein carry features incompatible with the secretory pathway or with luminal stability?

**Method:**
- SignalP 6.0 on the SP–uricase junction; confirm predicted cleavage site and that no cryptic site is generated by the fusion.
- DeepTMHMM / Phobius for spurious TM segments.
- Scan the CDS for a C-terminal PTS1 (last 3 residues; consensus [SAC][KRH][LM]) — if present, the protein will be mis-sorted to peroxisomes even with an N-terminal SP. **This is the single highest-probability failure mode of the current design.**
- NetNGlyc / NetOGlyc: *A. oryzae* hyperglycosylates; map N-X-S/T sites near the tetrameric interface (uricase is an obligate homotetramer; PDB 1R51-family structures) using AlphaFold2 + symmetry docking.
- Codon Adaptation Index vs. *A. oryzae* high-expression reference set; %MinMax to detect rare-codon clusters and over-optimization that removes co-translational folding pauses.
- ProteinMPNN / Rosetta ddG on predicted N-glycan sites at the dimer-dimer interface to estimate disruption risk.
- In silico proteolytic susceptibility (trypsin/chymotrypsin/pancreatic elastase) of the mature sequence using ExPASy PeptideCutter — a sanity check that a luminal enzyme is not shredded in the first 10 minutes post-pylorus.

**Decision informed:**
- PTS1 present → **modify**: append C-terminal extension (e.g., -SKL→-SKLG) or truncate to abolish peroxisomal targeting before any cloning.
- N-glycan at oligomerization interface → **modify**: design N→Q silent mutants for pilot panel.
- CAI <0.7 or major rare-codon cluster → **modify** codon table.
- All clear → **proceed** to #2.

**Biggest interpretability risk:** SignalP and glycosylation predictors are trained mostly on mammalian/yeast data; *Aspergillus* SP performance is reasonable but glycosite occupancy predictions are qualitative. A "clean" prediction does not mean clean experimental behavior — but a *failed* prediction (PTS1, TM domain) is highly actionable.

### 2. Heterologous small-scale expression in *Pichia pastoris* (or *S. cerevisiae*) with the same SP swap, as a secretion surrogate
**Cost: mid wet-lab, ~$1,500–3,000 · Timeline: 4–6 weeks**

**Question:** Does the codon-adjusted mature uricase fold, tetramerize, and secrete as an active enzyme when driven through a eukaryotic secretory pathway at all? I.e., is the *protein* secretable in principle, before committing to *A. oryzae* transformation cycles (which are slow: protoplasting, 7–10 day selection, single-spore purification).

**Method:**
- Synthesize two constructs: α-factor-SP–uricase in pPIC9K (or pPICZαA) and a no-SP intracellular control. Use *Pichia* codon optimization, not *A. oryzae* — the question is pathway compatibility, not host-matched expression.
- BMGY/BMMY shake-flask, 72 h methanol induction (or use a constitutive GAP variant to avoid methanol).
- Assay supernatant by (a) uricase activity (uric acid → allantoin, ΔA293, ε = 12.2 mM⁻¹cm⁻¹, ~$50 in reagents) and (b) SDS-PAGE ± PNGase F to assess glycosylation burden.
- Optional: pH 6.5 and pH 7.5 activity, with and without 10 mM taurocholate, to get a first read on luminal stability.

**Decision informed:**
- No detectable secreted activity, intracellular activity present → secretion path is the problem; **modify** SP (try native *A. niger* glucoamylase SP, or *A. oryzae* Taka-amylase pre-pro region instead of pre-only).
- No activity anywhere → folding/tetramerization is the problem; **kill** the heterologous-secretion approach, consider cytosolic expression + cell lysate formulation, or revert to a uricase ortholog with a secretion precedent (*Candida utilis*, *Bacillus* uricases).
- Active secreted enzyme, hyperglycosylated but functional → **proceed** to *A. oryzae*, with awareness that *A. oryzae* glycosylation will differ but is generally less extreme than *Pichia*.

**Biggest interpretability risk:** *Pichia* secretory pathway is not *A. oryzae*'s. A negative result in *Pichia* could falsely kill a construct that would have worked in *A. oryzae* (which secretes amylases at g/L). Mitigate by interpreting only **positive** results as strongly predictive; treat negatives as "investigate further," not "kill."

### 3. In vitro digestive stability of recombinant uricase (purchased or expressed)
**Cost: low-to-mid wet-lab, ~$400–1,000 · Timeline: 1–2 weeks**

**Question:** Given the intended GI delivery, what fraction of enzymatic activity survives a simulated intestinal fluid (SIF) challenge over a physiologically relevant residence time? This sets the upper bound on dose feasibility regardless of expression yield.

**Method:**
- Source: either commercial *A. flavus* uricase (Sigma U0880-class, ~$200/100 U) or supernatant from experiment #2 if available. Using commercial enzyme decouples this question from the expression question.
- INFOGEST 2.0 static digestion protocol — gastric phase optional if formulation is enteric-coated; focus on intestinal phase (pancreatin, bile salts, pH 7.0, 37°C, 120 min).
- Time-course aliquots into uricase activity assay (same ΔA293 readout).
- Control: BSA spike to confirm protease activity of the SIF.

**Decision informed:**
- <5% activity retained at 60 min → **kill or pivot**: even perfect expression won't deliver therapeutic activity orally without encapsulation; reassess whole product concept (encapsulation, crosslinking, PEGylation — but PEGylation breaks the "food-grade" framing).
- 5–50% retained → **modify** formulation strategy and recompute required expression titer to hit dose.
- >50% retained → **proceed**, expression yield is the gating factor not stability.

**Biggest interpretability risk:** Commercial uricase is purified from the native organism with native (not *A. oryzae*) glycosylation; the actual product's glycoform may have different proteolytic susceptibility. Result is therefore a *necessary but not sufficient* go-signal. A pass here doesn't guarantee the engineered product passes; a fail almost certainly means the engineered product also fails.

## Ordering justification

Information-per-dollar ranking is driven by (i) marginal cost, (ii) probability the experiment detects a project-killing flaw, and (iii) actionability.

**#1 dominates** because it is free, fast, and catches the highest-prior failure mode (C-terminal PTS1 mis-sorting of a peroxisomal enzyme through a secretory cassette). One afternoon avoids potentially months of unexplained "no secretion" troubleshooting.

**#2 outranks #3** despite higher cost because it tests the central, untested assumption of the project (that this enzyme is secretable as an active tetramer at all) using a faster host than *A. oryzae*. A negative result here saves the entire strain-engineering cycle. #3 ranks lowest only because the answer can be approximated from existing literature on oral uricase formulations (rasburicase is IV precisely because oral delivery of uricase is hard); the experiment confirms the magnitude but is least likely to change the go/no-go relative to prior expectation.
