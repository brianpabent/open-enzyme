# Stability and functional consequences of disulfide bond engineering in Aspergillus flavus uricase

**Authors:** Mohammad Reza Rahbar, Navid Nezafat, Mohammad Hossein Morowvat, Amir Savardashtaki, Mohammad Bagher Ghoshoon, Mohammad Soroosh Hajizade, Younes Ghasemi  
**Journal:** Scientific Reports, Volume 15, Issue 1, Article 18419  
**Year:** 2025  
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1038/s41598-025-01683-y  
**PubMed:** https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/40419569/

## Abstract

Disulfide bond engineering represents a strategy for enhancing enzyme stability in therapeutic and industrial applications. This investigation applied computational modeling to introduce interchain disulfide bonds in Aspergillus flavus uricase while preserving catalytic efficiency. Six enzyme variants were engineered with targeted disulfide bonds at positions determined through energetic frustration, structural integrity, and tunnel profiling analyses. Using frustration density mapping, Root Mean Square Fluctuation profiling, and tunnel analysis, the team evaluated structural stability, flexibility, and substrate accessibility. Results demonstrated that variants with disulfide bonds between residues including Ala6-Cys290 and Ser119-Cys220 showed significant reductions in highly frustrated regions, enhancing structural resilience. RMSF analysis indicated decreased local flexibility near disulfide sites. Tunnel profiling demonstrated that strategically positioned disulfide bonds maintained favorable substrate access and low-energy barriers for catalytic turnover. These findings underscore the potential of targeted disulfide bond engineering for optimizing enzyme stability in biocatalyst development.

## Relevance to Open Enzyme

This paper provides the direct computational-to-experimental workflow for engineering stability into A. flavus uricase — the same species background used in rasburicase (the FDA-approved IV uricase). The frustration-density plus RMSF plus tunnel-profiling three-part analysis is a replicable methodology for evaluating any uricase variant considered for the Open Enzyme platform. The key design constraint — disulfide bonds that increase thermostability without occluding the substrate-access tunnel — applies equally to stabilizing uricases for gut-lumen expression, where the enzyme faces proteolytic and pH stresses. The Ala6-Cys290 and Ser119-Cys220 disulfide positions are candidate modifications for the A. flavus uricase scaffold if that is selected over the Vibrio vulnificus enzyme.
