# Tranilast directly targets NLRP3 to treat inflammasome-driven diseases

**Authors:** Yi Huang, Hua Jiang, Yun Chen, Xiaqiong Wang, Yanqing Yang, Jinhui Tao, Xianming Deng, Gaolin Liang, Huafeng Zhang, Wei Jiang, Rongbin Zhou  
**Journal:** EMBO Molecular Medicine, Volume 10, Issue 4, Article e8689  
**Year:** 2018  
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.15252/emmm.201708689  
**PubMed:** https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/29531021/

## Abstract

The authors demonstrate that dysregulation of the NLRP3 inflammasome leads to uncontrolled inflammation and contributes to various human diseases, yet clinical medications targeting this pathway remain unavailable. Their research reveals that tranilast (TR), an established anti-allergic pharmaceutical, functions as a direct NLRP3 inhibitor. The compound selectively suppresses NLRP3 inflammasome activation in macrophages without affecting AIM2 or NLRC4 inflammasomes. Mechanistically, TR binds directly to the NACHT domain of NLRP3 and prevents inflammasome assembly by blocking oligomerization. In vivo studies demonstrate significant preventive and therapeutic efficacy in mouse models of NLRP3-related conditions including gouty arthritis, cryopyrin-associated autoinflammatory syndromes, and type 2 diabetes. Additionally, the compound showed effectiveness ex vivo using synovial cells from gout patients, suggesting potential clinical applicability.

## Relevance to Open Enzyme

Tranilast is an approved (Japan, Australia) anti-allergic drug with decades of human safety data, and this paper establishes it as a direct, selective NLRP3 NACHT-domain inhibitor with demonstrated in vivo efficacy in gouty arthritis models and ex vivo efficacy in human gout synovial cells. The repurposing angle makes tranilast an actionable adjunct for flare management alongside enzymatic urate reduction — lower regulatory and safety burden than a novel small molecule. For the Open Enzyme NLRP3 exploit map, tranilast represents the "existing drug, gout-validated" node in the NACHT-domain inhibitor category, complementing the covalent Cys279-targeting mechanism of oridonin.
