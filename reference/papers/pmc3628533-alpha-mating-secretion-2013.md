# The Effect of α-Mating Factor Secretion Signal Mutations on Recombinant Protein Expression in Pichia pastoris

**Authors:** Geoff P. Lin-Cereghino, Carolyn M. Stark, Daniel Kim, Jennifer Chang, Nadia Shaheen, Hansel Poerwanto, Kimiko Agari, Pachai Moua, Lauren K. Low, Namphuong Tran, Amy D. Huang, Maria Nattestad, Kristin T. Oshiro, John William Chang, Archana Chavan, Jerry W. Tsai, Joan Lin-Cereghino  
**Journal:** Gene, Volume 519, Issue 2, Pages 311–317  
**Year:** 2013  
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1016/j.gene.2013.01.062  
**PubMed:** https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/23454485/

## Abstract

The methylotrophic yeast Pichia pastoris has been engineered to produce numerous heterologous proteins. To facilitate protein secretion, recombinant coding sequences are fused to the Saccharomyces cerevisiae α-mating factor secretion signal. Through extensive site-directed mutagenesis of the prepro region, the authors evaluated how deletions and substitutions affect expression levels. While certain mutations reduced protein expression, deletion of amino acids 57-70 (corresponding to the predicted third alpha helix) substantially increased secretion of both horseradish peroxidase and lipase reporter proteins by at least 50% in small-scale cultures. These findings suggest the secretory efficiency of this leader sequence could be further optimized.

## Relevance to Open Enzyme

This paper is cited in the codon optimization analysis as a reference for secretion signal engineering in yeast expression systems. The α-mating factor prepro signal from S. cerevisiae is widely used across yeast platforms (S. cerevisiae, P. pastoris, S. boulardii) to drive protein secretion. The finding that deletion of the third alpha helix (aa 57–70) increases secretion by ≥50% is directly actionable: if Open Enzyme's uricase design requires secretion into the gut lumen (rather than cytoplasmic expression with cell lysis/leakage), the optimized α-MF prepro variant should be considered over the wild-type signal. Relevant to any therapeutic enzyme that needs to reach the extracellular space or luminal compartment.
